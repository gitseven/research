Question

A planet orbits Sun with period 2 years at distance 2 AU. Find period at distance 4 AU.

Background Concept — Kepler's Laws

Kepler's third law: T² ∝ r³ or T₁²/T₂² = r₁³/r₂³. This relates orbital period to semi-major axis. For circular orbits, r is the radius. The law applies to all planets orbiting the same central body.

Solution

T₁²/T₂² = r₁³/r₂³. (2)²/T₂² = (2)³/(4)³. 4/T₂² = 8/64 = 1/8. T₂² = 32. T₂ = √32 = 5.66 years

Tips to Solve

  • Use T₁²/T₂² = r₁³/r₂³
  • Substitute given values
  • Solve for unknown period
  • Check units (years)
  • Verify with T² ∝ r³

Key Formulas
