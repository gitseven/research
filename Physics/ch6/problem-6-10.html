Question

Calculate Schwarzschild radius for a 10 solar mass black hole. (M_sun = 1.99×10³⁰ kg)

Background Concept — Black Holes

Schwarzschild radius is the radius at which escape velocity equals speed of light. R_s = 2GM/c². Within this radius, nothing can escape, not even light. This defines the event horizon of a black hole. The radius is proportional to mass.

Solution

M = 10M_sun = 10×1.99×10³⁰ = 1.99×10³¹ kg. R_s = 2GM/c² = 2×6.67×10⁻¹¹×1.99×10³¹/(3×10⁸)² = 2.95×10⁴ m = 29.5 km

Tips to Solve

  • Use R_s = 2GM/c²
  • Calculate total mass
  • Use c = 3×10⁸ m/s
  • Check units (m)
  • Convert to km

Key Formulas

R_s = 2GM/c²
