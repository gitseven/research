Chapter 7 · Problem 10

Question

A 2 kg iron block at 100°C is dropped into 5 kg water at 20°C. Find final temperature. (c_iron = 450 J/kg⋅K, c_water = 4200 J/kg⋅K)

Background Concept — Heat Transfer

Heat transfer occurs until thermal equilibrium. Heat lost by hot object = Heat gained by cold object. Q = mcΔT where m is mass, c is specific heat, and ΔT is temperature change. Conservation of energy applies.

Solution

Heat lost by iron = Heat gained by water. m₁c₁(T₁ - T_f) = m₂c₂(T_f - T₂). 2×450×(100 - T_f) = 5×4200×(T_f - 20). 900×(100 - T_f) = 21000×(T_f - 20). 90000 - 900T_f = 21000T_f - 420000. 510000 = 21900T_f. T_f = 23.3°C

Tips to Solve

  • Apply heat conservation
  • Use Q = mcΔT
  • Set heat lost = heat gained
  • Solve for final temperature
  • Check units (°C)

Key Formulas

Q = mcΔT, Heat lost = Heat gained
