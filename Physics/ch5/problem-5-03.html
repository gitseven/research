Chapter 5 · Problem 3

Question

A particle of mass 0.5 kg moves in a circle of radius 2 m with speed 4 m/s. Find angular momentum about center.

Background Concept — Angular Momentum

Angular momentum L = r × p = rmv sinθ. For circular motion, L = rmv = mr²ω. It's conserved in absence of external torque. Angular momentum is a vector quantity with direction given by right-hand rule.

Solution

L = rmv = (2)(0.5)(4) = 4 kg·m²/s. Since motion is circular, L = mr²ω = (0.5)(2)²(2) = 4 kg·m²/s. Angular momentum = 4 kg·m²/s

Tips to Solve

  • Use L = rmv for linear motion
  • Apply L = mr²ω for circular motion
  • Check units (kg·m²/s)
  • Consider direction (perpendicular to plane)
  • Verify with ω = v/r

Key Formulas

L = r × p = rmv, L = mr²ω
