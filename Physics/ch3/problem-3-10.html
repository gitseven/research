Chapter 3 · Problem 10

Question

A rocket of mass 1000 kg ejects 100 kg at 200 m/s. Find final velocity if initial velocity was 50 m/s.

Background Concept — Rocket Propulsion

Rocket propulsion uses conservation of momentum. Initial momentum = final momentum. Consider ejected mass and remaining mass.

Solution

Initial p = (1000)(50) = 50,000 kg⋅m/s. Final: (900)v + (100)(200) = 50,000. 900v = 30,000. v = 33.3 m/s

Tips to Solve

  • Apply momentum conservation
  • Consider ejected mass
  • Use proper signs
  • Check units
  • Verify total momentum

Key Formulas

m₁v₁ = m₂v₂ + m₃v₃
