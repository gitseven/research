Chapter 2 · Problem 6

Question

A ball is thrown upward with velocity 20 m/s. Find: (a) maximum height, (b) time to reach maximum height, (c) total time in air.

Background Concept — Motion Under Gravity

Motion under gravity involves constant acceleration g = 9.8 m/s² downward. At maximum height, velocity becomes zero. Time to go up equals time to come down. Use v = v₀ + at and h = v₀t + ½at².

Solution

(a) At max height, v = 0. Using v² = v₀² + 2ah: 0 = 20² + 2(-9.8)h → h = 20.4 m. (b) Using v = v₀ + at: 0 = 20 + (-9.8)t → t = 2.04 s. (c) Total time = 2 × 2.04 = 4.08 s

Tips to Solve

  • At max height, v = 0
  • Use v² = v₀² + 2ah for height
  • Apply v = v₀ + at for time
  • Total time = 2 × time to max height
  • Check with h = v₀t - ½gt²

Key Formulas

h_max = v₀²/(2g), t_up = v₀/g, t_total = 2v₀/g
