Chapter 2 · Problem 5

Question

A stone is dropped from a height of 45 m. Find: (a) time to reach ground, (b) velocity on impact, (c) distance fallen in last second.

Background Concept — Free Fall

Free fall is motion under gravity only. Use kinematic equations with a = g = 9.8 m/s². For dropped objects, initial velocity v₀ = 0. Distance in last second = total distance - distance in (t-1) seconds.

Solution

(a) Using h = ½gt²: 45 = ½(9.8)t² → t = 3.03 s. (b) v = gt = 9.8(3.03) = 29.7 m/s. (c) Distance in 2.03 s = ½(9.8)(2.03)² = 20.2 m. Last second = 45 - 20.2 = 24.8 m

Tips to Solve

  • Use h = ½gt² for time
  • Apply v = gt for final velocity
  • Calculate distance in (t-1) seconds
  • Subtract from total height
  • Check with v² = 2gh

Key Formulas

h = ½gt², v = gt, v² = 2gh
