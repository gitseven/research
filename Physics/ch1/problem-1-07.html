Chapter 1 · Problem 7

Question

Estimate the order of magnitude of the number of atoms in a human body. (Mass of human ≈ 70 kg, mass of atom ≈ 10⁻²⁶ kg)

Background Concept — Order of Magnitude

Order of magnitude estimation helps understand the scale of physical quantities. It involves rounding to the nearest power of 10. This is useful for checking if answers are reasonable and for quick approximations in complex problems.

Visualization

Solution

Number of atoms = mass of body/mass of atom = 70 kg / 10⁻²⁶ kg = 7 × 10²⁷. Order of magnitude = 10²⁸ atoms. This means there are approximately 10²⁸ atoms in a human body.

Tips to Solve

  • Round numbers to nearest power of 10
  • Use scientific notation
  • Compare with known values
  • Check if result is reasonable
  • Express as 10ⁿ format

Key Formulas

Order of magnitude = 10ⁿ where n is the exponent
