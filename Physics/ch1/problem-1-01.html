Chapter 1 · Problem 1

Question

Express the following measurements in scientific notation with appropriate significant figures: (a) 0.000000000154 m (b) 0.000000000000000000000000000000910938356 kg (c) 299792458 m/s

Background Concept — Physical World and Measurement

This problem involves fundamental concepts from Chapter 1: Physical World and Measurement. Understanding these concepts is crucial for solving physics problems systematically.

Measurement and Units

Measurement and Units: Physics is fundamentally about measuring physical quantities. The International System of Units (SI) provides seven base units: meter (m) for length, kilogram (kg) for mass, second (s) for time, ampere (A) for electric current, kelvin (K) for temperature, mole (mol) for amount of substance, and candela (cd) for luminous intensity. All other units are derived from these base units. For example, velocity is measured in m/s, acceleration in m/s², and force in kg⋅m/s² = newton (N).

Dimensional Analysis: Every physical quantity has dimensions expressed in terms of fundamental quantities [M], [L], [T] for mass, length, and time respectively. Dimensional analysis helps verify equations and derive relationships between physical quantities. The principle of homogeneity states that both sides of an equation must have the same dimensions.

Visualization

Solution

(a) 0.000000000154 m
This is the radius of a hydrogen atom. In scientific notation: 1.54 × 10⁻¹⁰ m
Significant figures: 3 (1, 5, 4)

(b) 0.000000000000000000000000000000910938356 kg
This is the mass of an electron. In scientific notation: 9.10938356 × 10⁻³¹ kg
Significant figures: 9 (all digits are significant)

(c) 299792458 m/s
This is the speed of light in vacuum. In scientific notation: 2.99792458 × 10⁸ m/s
Significant figures: 9 (all digits are significant)

Tips to Solve

  • Always check units and dimensions - they must be consistent throughout
  • Use significant figures rules: multiplication/division uses least sig figs, addition/subtraction uses least decimal places
  • Convert all quantities to SI units before calculations
  • Estimate answers to check if they're reasonable
  • Use dimensional analysis to verify derived formulas
