Chapter 1 · Problem 10

Question

The speed of light in vacuum is c = 2.99792458 × 10⁸ m/s. Express this value with 3 significant figures and calculate the time for light to travel 1 km.

Background Concept — Physical Constants

Physical constants have high precision and are fundamental to physics. The speed of light is exactly 299,792,458 m/s by definition. When using constants in calculations, maintain appropriate significant figures based on the precision of other measurements in the problem.

Visualization

Solution

c = 2.99792458 × 10⁸ m/s ≈ 3.00 × 10⁸ m/s (3 significant figures). Time = distance/speed = 1000 m / (3.00 × 10⁸ m/s) = 3.33 × 10⁻⁶ s = 3.33 μs.

Tips to Solve

  • Use appropriate significant figures
  • Apply t = d/v formula
  • Convert units as needed
  • Check order of magnitude
  • Express in convenient units

Key Formulas

c = 3.00 × 10⁸ m/s, t = d/v
