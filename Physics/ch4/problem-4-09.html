Chapter 4 · Problem 9

Question

Two 2 kg balls collide. Before: v₁ = 5 m/s, v₂ = -3 m/s. After: v₁ = -2 m/s, v₂ = 4 m/s. Is kinetic energy conserved?

Background Concept — Collision Energy

In elastic collisions, both momentum and kinetic energy are conserved. In inelastic collisions, only momentum is conserved. Check if total KE before = total KE after.

Solution

KE before = ½(2)(5)² + ½(2)(-3)² = 25 + 9 = 34 J. KE after = ½(2)(-2)² + ½(2)(4)² = 4 + 16 = 20 J. Not conserved (inelastic)

Tips to Solve

  • Calculate KE before and after
  • Compare totals
  • If equal: elastic
  • If different: inelastic
  • Check momentum too

Key Formulas

KE = ½mv²
