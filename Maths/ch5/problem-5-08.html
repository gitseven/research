Question

Find the derivative of f(x) = xˣ.

Background Concept — Logarithmic Differentiation

Logarithmic differentiation is used for functions of the form f(x) = g(x)ʰ⁽ˣ⁾. We take the natural logarithm of both sides and then differentiate. This method is useful when both the base and exponent are functions of x. The key steps are: take ln, differentiate, solve for f'(x).

Solution

Taking natural logarithm: ln(f(x)) = ln(xˣ) = x ln(x). Differentiating both sides: (1/f(x))f'(x) = d/dx[x ln(x)] = 1·ln(x) + x·(1/x) = ln(x) + 1. Therefore f'(x) = f(x)(ln(x) + 1) = xˣ(ln(x) + 1).

Tips to Solve

  • Use properties of logarithms
  • Differentiate both sides
  • Solve for f'(x)
  • Substitute back for f(x)
