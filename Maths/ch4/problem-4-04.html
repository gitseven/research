Chapter 4 · Problem 4

Question

Find the adjoint of A = [[1,2],[3,4]].

Background Concept — Adjoint Matrix

The adjoint (or adjugate) of a matrix A is the transpose of the cofactor matrix. For a 2×2 matrix [[a,b],[c,d]], the adjoint is [[d,-b],[-c,a]]. The adjoint is related to the inverse: A⁻¹ = (1/det(A))adj(A). The adjoint is useful in finding inverses and solving systems.

Solution

For A = [[1,2],[3,4]], the cofactor matrix is [[4,-3],[-2,1]]. Taking the transpose: adj(A) = [[4,-2],[-3,1]].

Tips to Solve

  • Find the cofactor matrix
  • Take the transpose
  • For 2×2: adj([[a,b],[c,d]]) = [[d,-b],[-c,a]]
  • Check with A·adj(A) = det(A)I
  • Use for finding inverse

Key Formulas

adj(A) = transpose of cofactor matrix; A⁻¹ = (1/det(A))adj(A)
