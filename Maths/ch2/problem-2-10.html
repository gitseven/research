Chapter 2 · Problem 10

Question

A ladder 10 m long leans against a wall. If the foot of the ladder is 6 m from the wall, find the angle the ladder makes with the ground.

Background Concept — Applications

Inverse trigonometric functions are used to find angles in right triangles. Given two sides, we can use inverse trigonometric functions to find the angle. For a right triangle with adjacent side a and hypotenuse h, the angle θ satisfies cos θ = a/h, so θ = cos⁻¹(a/h).

Solution

In the right triangle formed by the ladder, wall, and ground, the adjacent side is 6 m and the hypotenuse is 10 m. The angle θ satisfies cos θ = 6/10 = 0.6. Therefore θ = cos⁻¹(0.6) ≈ 53.13°.

Tips to Solve

  • Identify the right triangle
  • Use cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse
  • Apply cos⁻¹ to find angle
  • Check the answer makes sense
  • Use calculator for numerical value

Key Formulas

cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse; θ = cos⁻¹(adjacent/hypotenuse)
