Electrochemistry · Problem 3.16

Original Question

Three electrolytic cells A, B, C containing solutions of ZnSO₄, AgNO₃ and CuSO₄, respectively are connected in series. A steady current of 1.5 amperes was passed until 1.45 g of silver deposited at the cathode of cell B. How long did the current flow? What mass of copper and zinc were deposited?

Background Concept — Same Current in Series; Use Equivalent Mass

In series, the same current passes through all cells for the same time. Moles of Ag deposited: n(Ag) = 1.45/107.87 ≈ 0.01344 mol. For Ag⁺ + e⁻ → Ag, 1 e⁻ per Ag; hence moles of electrons passed = 0.01344 mol, charge Q = nF ≈ 0.01344×96485 ≈ 1297 C. Time t = Q/I = 1297/1.5 ≈ 865 s ≈ 14.4 min. For Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu, moles Cu = (moles e⁻)/2 ≈ 0.00672 mol; mass Cu ≈ 0.00672×63.55 ≈ 0.427 g. For Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Zn, moles Zn = (moles e⁻)/2 ≈ 0.00672 mol; mass Zn ≈ 0.00672×65.38 ≈ 0.439 g. This approach generalizes by using electrochemical equivalents (M/z) and the fact that identical charge passes through series cells. Always verify oxidation states and electron counts (z) for each metal ion; Ag is monovalent here; Cu and Zn are divalent in the given salts. Note: small rounding differences arise from atomic masses used.

Answer

t ≈ 865 s ≈ 14.4 min
m(Cu) ≈ 0.427 g; m(Zn) ≈ 0.439 g
