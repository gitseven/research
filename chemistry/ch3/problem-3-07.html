Chapter 3 · Problem 7

Question

Explain the mechanism of rusting of iron and suggest two methods to prevent it.

Background Concept — Corrosion and Prevention

Rusting is an electrochemical process where iron acts as anode and gets oxidized to Fe²⁺, while oxygen acts as cathode and gets reduced to OH⁻. The overall reaction is 4Fe + 3O₂ + 6H₂O → 4Fe(OH)₃. Prevention methods include galvanizing, painting, cathodic protection, and alloying. The process requires both oxygen and water.

Visualization

Solution

Mechanism: Anode: Fe → Fe²⁺ + 2e⁻. Cathode: O₂ + 2H₂O + 4e⁻ → 4OH⁻. Overall: 4Fe + 3O₂ + 6H₂O → 4Fe(OH)₃ (rust). Prevention methods: (1) Galvanizing: Coating with zinc, (2) Painting: Creating a barrier to oxygen and water, (3) Cathodic protection: Connecting to a more reactive metal, (4) Alloying: Making stainless steel.

Tips to Solve

  • Write electrochemical half-reactions
  • Identify anode and cathode
  • Explain the role of oxygen and water
  • Suggest practical prevention methods
  • Consider cost and effectiveness
