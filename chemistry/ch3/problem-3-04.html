Chapter 3 · Problem 4

Question

The resistance of 0.1 M KCl solution in a conductivity cell is 100 Ω. If the cell constant is 0.1 cm⁻¹, calculate the conductivity and molar conductivity of the solution.

Background Concept — Conductance and Molar Conductivity

Conductivity (κ) is the reciprocal of resistivity and depends on the cell constant (G). κ = G/R, where G is cell constant and R is resistance. Molar conductivity (Λm) = κ/C, where C is concentration. Molar conductivity increases with dilution due to increased ionization. The unit of conductivity is S cm⁻¹.

Visualization

Solution

Conductivity κ = G/R = 0.1/100 = 0.001 S cm⁻¹. Molar conductivity Λm = κ/C = 0.001/0.1 = 0.01 S cm² mol⁻¹. Therefore, conductivity = 0.001 S cm⁻¹ and molar conductivity = 0.01 S cm² mol⁻¹.

Tips to Solve

  • Use κ = G/R for conductivity
  • Calculate Λm = κ/C for molar conductivity
  • Check units carefully
  • Convert concentration to mol/L
  • Verify with known values

Key Formulas

κ = G/R; Λm = κ/C; G = cell constant, R = resistance, C = concentration
