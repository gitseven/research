Electrochemistry · Problem 3.12

Original Question

The amount of charge required for the reduction of 1 mol of MnO₄⁻ to Mn²⁺ is: (i) 1 F (ii) 3 F (iii) 5 F (iv) 6 F

Background Concept — Electron Stoichiometry and Faraday

Faraday’s laws relate the charge passed to the moles of electrons transferred: 1 faraday (F) ≈ 96485 C corresponds to 1 mol e⁻. In acidic medium, the balanced reduction half‑reaction for permanganate is: MnO₄⁻ + 8H⁺ + 5e⁻ → Mn²⁺ + 4H₂O. Thus, 5 moles of electrons are required per mole of MnO₄⁻ to form Mn²⁺. Therefore, the charge needed is 5F for 1 mol MnO₄⁻. In neutral or basic media, the reduction products differ (e.g., MnO₂ or MnO₄²⁻), but the question specifically targets Mn²⁺, which is the acidic reduction product requiring five electrons. This illustrates the importance of knowing the correct balanced half‑reaction and the reaction medium. The general approach: determine electrons per mole of reactant from the half‑reaction, then multiply by Faraday’s constant to get charge. This also underpins coulometric titrations and electroplating calculations, where current and time are used to quantify substance amounts.

Answer

Electrons per MnO₄⁻ → Mn²⁺ (acid): 5 e⁻ → Charge = 5 F → (iii)
