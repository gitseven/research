Electrochemistry · Problem 3.17

Original Question

Using the standard electrode potentials given in Table 3.1, predict if the reaction between the following is feasible: (i) Fe³⁺(aq) and I⁻(aq) (ii) Ag⁺(aq) and Cu(s) (iii) Fe³⁺(aq) and Br⁻(aq) (iv) Ag(s) and Fe³⁺(aq) (v) Br₂(aq) and Fe²⁺(aq)

Background Concept — Cell EMF from E° and Feasibility

A redox reaction under standard conditions is feasible if the associated cell potential E°cell = E°(cathode) − E°(anode) is positive. Use standard reduction potentials: species with higher (more positive) E° are better oxidants (more readily reduced). For a proposed reaction “A oxidizes B,” assign reductions and oxidations, subtract potentials, and check the sign. As examples, the couples: Ag⁺/Ag (+0.80 V), Fe³⁺/Fe²⁺ (+0.77 V), Br₂/Br⁻ (+1.07 V), I₂/I⁻ (+0.54 V), Cu²⁺/Cu (+0.34 V) suggest halogens are strong oxidants in the order Br₂ > I₂, and Ag⁺ can oxidize Cu(s) to Cu²⁺ since +0.80 − (+0.34) > 0. By contrast, Fe³⁺ vs I⁻ compares +0.77 (Fe³⁺/Fe²⁺) with +0.54 (I₂/I⁻), giving positive E° when Fe³⁺ is reduced and I⁻ is oxidized to I₂.

Answer (using common E° values)

  • (i) Fe³⁺ + I⁻ → Fe²⁺ + ½I₂: E° ≈ +0.77 − (+0.54) = +0.23 V → feasible.
  • (ii) Ag⁺ + Cu(s) → Ag(s) + Cu²⁺: E° ≈ +0.80 − (+0.34) = +0.46 V → feasible.
  • (iii) Fe³⁺ + Br⁻ → Fe²⁺ + ½Br₂: E° ≈ +0.77 − (+1.07) = −0.30 V → not feasible.
  • (iv) Fe³⁺ + Ag(s) → Fe²⁺ + Ag⁺: E° ≈ +0.77 − (+0.80) = −0.03 V → not feasible.
  • (v) Br₂ + Fe²⁺ → 2Br⁻ + Fe³⁺: E° ≈ +1.07 − (+0.77) = +0.30 V → feasible.
Criterion: E°cell > 0 → spontaneous (feasible).
