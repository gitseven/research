Electrochemistry · Problem 3.14

Original Question

The amount of electricity (in coulombs) required for the oxidation of: (i) 1 mol of H₂O to O₂ (ii) 1 mol of FeO to Fe₂O₃

Background Concept — Electrons per Reaction and Q = nF

The total charge needed (Q) equals the moles of electrons transferred multiplied by Faraday’s constant, Q = n(e⁻) × F, with F ≈ 96485 C mol⁻¹. Determine electrons per mole of reactant from balanced redox changes. For water to oxygen in acidic medium: 2H₂O → O₂ + 4H⁺ + 4e⁻, showing 4 electrons are produced per mole of O₂ formed; conversely, to form O₂ from H₂O by electrolysis, 4 electrons must be removed per O₂. For iron(II) oxide to iron(III) oxide, Fe oxidation state increases from +2 in FeO to +3 in Fe₂O₃. Two Fe²⁺ become two Fe³⁺ in Fe₂O₃, losing a total of 2 electrons per mole of Fe₂O₃ formed from 2 mol FeO. Therefore, for 1 mol FeO oxidized to Fe₂O₃ stoichiometrically, only 1 electron is involved per Fe (but watch stoichiometry). A convenient route is to write: 2FeO → Fe₂O₃ + 2H⁺ + 2e⁻ (in acidic bookkeeping), indicating 2 electrons per Fe₂O₃ produced from 2 FeO. Hence per 1 mol FeO, 1 electron is required. Multiply electron moles by F to get coulombs. These calculations assume 100% current efficiency and no side reactions; real cells often require more charge due to overpotentials and inefficiencies, but the theoretical value is set by electron stoichiometry alone. Correct identification of oxidation states and precise stoichiometric balancing are the key steps to compute electron equivalents accurately.

Answer

(i) 2H₂O → O₂ + 4H⁺ + 4e⁻ → n(e⁻) = 4 per mol O₂ → Q = 4F ≈ 3.86 × 10⁵ C
(ii) 2FeO → Fe₂O₃ + 2H⁺ + 2e⁻ → n(e⁻) = 2 per 2 mol FeO → 1 e⁻ per 1 mol FeO → Q = 1F ≈ 9.65 × 10⁴ C

Tips to Solve

  • Balance for electrons; then Q = nF.
  • For FeO → Fe₂O₃, track Fe: +2 → +3 (one electron per Fe).
