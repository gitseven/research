Electrochemistry · Problem 3.15

Original Question

A solution of Ni(NO₃)₂ is electrolysed between platinum electrodes using a current of 5 amperes for 20 minutes. What mass of Ni is deposited at the cathode?

Background Concept — Faraday’s First Law

Charge passed Q = I × t (coulombs). Moles of electrons = Q/F, where F ≈ 96485 C mol⁻¹. For Ni²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Ni(s), two moles of electrons deposit one mole of Ni. Therefore, moles Ni = (Q/F)/2, and mass m = (Q/F) × (M/2), with Ni atomic mass M ≈ 58.69 g mol⁻¹. This calculation assumes 100% current efficiency with inert electrodes. Side reactions (e.g., H₂ evolution) or passivation reduce actual yield in practice, but theoretical yield follows stoichiometry directly from Faraday’s law. Using SI units, ensure time is in seconds and current in amperes to obtain Q in coulombs. The approach generalizes to other metal ions by replacing the electron stoichiometry and atomic mass accordingly.

Answer

Q = I t = 5 A × (20 × 60 s) = 6000 C
mol e⁻ = Q/F ≈ 6000/96485 ≈ 0.0622 mol; mol Ni = 0.0622/2 ≈ 0.0311 mol
m(Ni) = 0.0311 × 58.69 g mol⁻¹ ≈ 1.83 g

Approximately 1.83 g of Ni is deposited.

Tips to Solve

  • Compute Q = I t; convert minutes to seconds.
  • Divide moles of electrons by 2 for Ni²⁺.
