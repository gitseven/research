d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.30

Original Question

Which is the last element in the series of actinoids? Write the electronic configuration of this element. Comment on the possible oxidation state of this element.

Background Concept — End of the Actinoid Series

The actinoid series runs from actinium (Z = 89) to lawrencium (Z = 103). Lawrencium, Lr (Z = 103), is thus the last member. Its ground‑state configuration is generally represented as [Rn] 5f¹⁴ 7s² 7p¹ or [Rn] 5f¹⁴ 7s² 6d¹ depending on interpretation; recent spectroscopic evidence supports a 7p electron. In chemistry, however, lawrencium is expected to behave primarily as a +3 cation (Lr³⁺), similar to other late actinoids and lanthanoids, reflecting the dominant +3 oxidation state with limited participation of the filled 5f shell. Due to its short half‑life and the difficulty of preparing macroscopic quantities, its chemistry is inferred from trends and limited experiments rather than extensive bulk studies.

Answer

Last actinoid: Lawrencium (Lr), Z = 103.

Electronic configuration (common representation): [Rn] 5f¹⁴ 7s² 7p¹ (often historically written as [Rn] 5f¹⁴ 7s² 6d¹).

Likely oxidation state: +3 (Lr³⁺) predominates, analogous to late actinoids/lanthanoids.

Tips to Solve

  • Memorize actinoid range: 89–103; last is Lr.
  • Note modern configuration evidence favors 7p involvement.
  • State +3 as the predominant oxidation state.
