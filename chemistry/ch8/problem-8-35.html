d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.35

Original Question

Compare the general characteristics of the first, second and third series transition metals in vertical columns, with special emphasis on: (i) Electronic configurations (ii) Oxidation states (iii) Ionisation enthalpies (iv) Atomic sizes.

Background Concept — 3d vs 4d vs 5d Trends

Transition series down a group show relativistic and contraction effects. From 3d → 4d → 5d, atomic sizes do not increase much between 4d and 5d due to lanthanoid contraction, leading to similar radii (e.g., Zr ≈ Hf). Ionisation enthalpies generally rise across a period, but vertically 5d elements often have higher values due to relativistic stabilization of s and contraction effects. Oxidation states broaden in heavier series, with 5d showing higher maximum oxidation (e.g., Os +8) and greater preference for strong-field ligands and covalency. Electronic configurations maintain group trends but show anomalies tied to subshell stability and relativistic effects.

Answer

  • Electronic configurations: Group-wise similarities persist across 3d/4d/5d, but 5d shows relativistic shifts; d-occupancy trends remain comparable.
  • Oxidation states: Increase in variety and higher maxima down the group (5d > 4d > 3d). Strong-field stabilization more common in 5d.
  • Ionisation enthalpies: Generally higher for 5d vs 4d due to relativistic stabilization; irregularities occur with d/s interplay.
  • Atomic sizes: 4d > 3d; 5d ≈ 4d (lanthanoid contraction yields Zr ≈ Hf, Nb ≈ Ta).

Tips to Solve

  • Mention lanthanoid contraction for 4d vs 5d similarity.
  • Note higher oxidation states more common/stable in 5d.
  • Relate stronger ligand fields and covalency to heavier series.
