d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.27

Original Question

What are alloys? Name an important alloy which contains some of the lanthanoid metals. Mention its uses.

Background Concept — Alloys and Lanthanoid Additions

An alloy is a homogeneous mixture of two or more elements, at least one of which is a metal, formed to tailor properties such as strength, ductility, corrosion resistance, magnetic behavior, or melting point. Lanthanoid metals are often added in small amounts to improve grain refinement, remove oxygen or sulfur impurities (as scavengers), and impart magnetic or luminescent properties. Mischmetal is a key alloy consisting primarily of cerium and other light lanthanoids (La, Pr, Nd). It is used in flints for lighters due to its pyrophoric behavior upon friction (via cerium’s ease of oxidation). Lanthanoid additions also improve desulfurization and deoxidation in steel and cast iron, enhancing mechanical properties. Other lanthanoid-containing alloys include Al–Nd for high-temperature magnets and Sm–Co and Nd–Fe–B for permanent magnets with high coercivity and energy product, crucial in motors and electronics.

Answer

Alloy: A metallic material containing two or more elements, designed to achieve properties superior to the base metal(s).

Important lanthanoid alloy: Mischmetal (≈ 40–50% Ce with La, Nd, Pr).

  • Uses: Lighter flints (pyrophoric), steel deoxidizer/desulfurizer, starting material for rare-earth magnets.
  • Other examples: Nd–Fe–B and Sm–Co magnets for motors, HDDs, speakers; Al–Nd for high-temperature alloys.

  • List at least two uses (flints; steel refining; magnets).
