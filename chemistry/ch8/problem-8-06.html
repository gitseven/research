d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.6

Original Question

Name the oxometal anions of the first series transition metals in which the metal exhibits the oxidation state equal to its group number.

Background Concept — Highest Oxidation States in Oxoanions

Highest oxidation states of early transition metals are stabilised by strongly electronegative ligands, notably oxygen and fluorine. Multiple M=O bonds delocalise electron density and, together with lattice/solvation energies, stabilise high formal charges on the metal, often matching the periodic group number (for the 3d row: V(5), Cr(6), Mn(7)). Classic oxoanions such as vanadate VO₄³⁻ (V(V)), chromate CrO₄²⁻/dichromate Cr₂O₇²⁻ (Cr(VI)), and permanganate MnO₄⁻ (Mn(VII)) showcase this. The trend fades beyond Mn because forming even higher oxidation states becomes energetically costly and the requisite π bonding to oxygen is less favourable; later 3d metals prefer lower oxidation states with softer ligands. Recognising charge balance with oxygen (−2 each) allows quick oxidation‑state checks: in MnO₄⁻, 4×(−2) = −8 overall; with −1 charge, Mn must be +7. Similar reasoning gives +6 for Cr in CrO₄²⁻ and +5 for V in VO₄³⁻. These anions are powerful oxidants in aqueous solution, consistent with their high metal oxidation states and the thermodynamic driving force to form lower‑valent oxides or aquo complexes.

Answer

  • VO₄³⁻ (vanadate, V(V), group 5)
  • CrO₄²⁻ / Cr₂O₇²⁻ (chromate/dichromate, Cr(VI), group 6)
  • MnO₄⁻ (permanganate, Mn(VII), group 7)

Tips to Solve

  • Use charge balance with O²⁻ to deduce metal oxidation state.
  • Expect highest states with O/F ligands in early 3d metals.
  • Remember: vanadate (+5), chromate/dichromate (+6), permanganate (+7).
