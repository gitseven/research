d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.37

Original Question

Comment on the statement that elements of the first transition series possess many properties different from those of heavier transition elements.

Background Concept — 3d vs 4d/5d Differences

First-row (3d) metals differ from 4d/5d congeners due to smaller atomic/ionic sizes, weaker relativistic effects, and generally weaker ligand fields with more pronounced high-spin configurations. Heavier congeners (4d/5d) experience lanthanoid contraction (for 5d), stronger metal–ligand covalency, higher CFSE in strong fields (hence more low-spin complexes), and stabilize higher oxidation states more readily. These factors lead to differences in magnetic behavior, preferred geometries, catalytic properties, and redox trends across groups.

Answer

  • Spin states: 3d ions often high-spin with weak-field ligands; 4d/5d more often low-spin.
  • Oxidation states: Higher and more stable high oxidation states in 5d > 4d > 3d.
  • Covalency: 4d/5d show greater covalency and stronger ligand fields.
  • Sizes: 5d radii similar to 4d (lanthanoid contraction); both larger than 3d analogues.
  • Reactivity/catalysis: Heavier series often superior heterogeneous catalysts and form stable π-acceptor complexes.
