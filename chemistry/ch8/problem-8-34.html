d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.34

Original Question

Write the electronic configurations of the elements with atomic numbers 61, 91, 101, and 109.

Background Concept — f- and d-block Anomalies

Electronic configurations of f- and heavy d-block elements may show deviations due to relativistic effects and subshell energy near-degeneracies. Nonetheless, standard reported ground-state configurations provide reliable answers for problem-solving. Here we list commonly accepted configurations for promethium (61), protactinium (91), mendelevium (101), and meitnerium (109). Note that superheavy elements (e.g., Mt) are short-lived; configurations are inferred from periodic trends and calculations.

Answer

Z = 61 (Pm): [Xe] 4f⁵ 6s²
Z = 91 (Pa): [Rn] 5f² 6d¹ 7s²
Z = 101 (Md): [Rn] 5f¹³ 7s²
Z = 109 (Mt): [Rn] 5f¹⁴ 6d⁷ 7s²

These align with typical filling orders for 4f/5f and late 6d elements, acknowledging small variations reported in advanced texts.
