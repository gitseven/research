d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.21

Original Question

What is lanthanoid contraction? What are its consequences?

Background Concept — Lanthanoid Contraction Phenomenon

Lanthanoid contraction refers to the gradual decrease in atomic and ionic radii of lanthanoid elements (La to Lu) as the atomic number increases. This phenomenon occurs because the 4f electrons are poorly shielded from the nuclear charge, causing the effective nuclear charge to increase more rapidly than expected. As we move from La to Lu, the 4f subshell is progressively filled with 14 electrons, but these f-electrons are buried deep inside the atom and provide poor shielding for the outer electrons. This results in a steady increase in the effective nuclear charge experienced by the outer 6s electrons, causing them to be pulled closer to the nucleus. The contraction is approximately 1 pm per element, which is significant considering the small size of these atoms. This phenomenon has profound consequences on the properties of elements in the same group, particularly affecting the 5d transition elements that follow the lanthanoids. The lanthanoid contraction explains why elements like Hf and Zr have nearly identical atomic radii despite Hf having 32 more protons, and why the 4d and 5d transition series show similar chemical properties.

Answer

Lanthanoid Contraction: Gradual decrease in atomic/ionic radii of lanthanoids (La to Lu) due to poor shielding by 4f electrons.

Consequences:

  • Similar radii: 4d and 5d transition elements have nearly identical sizes
  • Chemical similarity: Zr and Hf, Nb and Ta, Mo and W show similar properties
  • Separation difficulty: Lanthanoids are difficult to separate due to similar properties
  • Ionic radii: Ln³⁺ ions show regular decrease in size across the series
  • Basic character: Decreases from La(OH)₃ to Lu(OH)₃ due to smaller ionic size
Example: La³⁺ (1.06 Å) → Lu³⁺ (0.85 Å) - 20% decrease

Tips to Solve

  • Remember: 4f electrons provide poor shielding due to their deep location.
  • Effective nuclear charge increases more rapidly than expected.
  • Contraction affects both atomic and ionic radii.
  • Consequences are most evident in 5d transition elements.
  • Lanthanoids become more acidic (less basic) across the series.
