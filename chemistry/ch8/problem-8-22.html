d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.22

Original Question

List the characteristic properties of transition elements.

Background Concept — Transition Element Characteristics

Transition elements are defined as those having partially filled d-orbitals in their ground state or in any of their common oxidation states. These elements exhibit unique properties due to the presence of d-electrons, which are responsible for their distinctive characteristics. The d-electrons provide multiple oxidation states, variable valency, and the ability to form colored compounds. Transition elements are typically metals with high melting and boiling points, high densities, and good electrical and thermal conductivity. They form complex ions and compounds due to their ability to act as Lewis acids and accept electron pairs from ligands. The d-electrons also contribute to magnetic properties, with many transition metals being paramagnetic due to unpaired electrons. These elements often act as catalysts in various chemical reactions due to their ability to change oxidation states easily. The atomic radii of transition elements decrease across a period due to increasing nuclear charge, but the decrease is less pronounced than in main group elements due to the shielding effect of d-electrons. The ionization energies increase gradually across the series, and the elements show metallic character with decreasing basicity of their oxides.

Answer

Characteristic Properties of Transition Elements:

  • Partially filled d-orbitals: Have incomplete d-subshell in ground state or common oxidation states
  • Multiple oxidation states: Can exhibit various oxidation states (e.g., Fe: +2, +3; Mn: +2, +3, +4, +6, +7)
  • Colored compounds: Form colored ions and complexes due to d-d transitions
  • Magnetic properties: Many are paramagnetic due to unpaired d-electrons
  • High melting/boiling points: Strong metallic bonding due to d-electrons
  • High densities: Close packing of atoms in crystal structures
  • Good conductors: High electrical and thermal conductivity
  • Catalytic activity: Act as catalysts due to variable oxidation states
  • Formation of complexes: Act as Lewis acids and form coordination compounds
  • Alloy formation: Form alloys with other metals
  • Interstitial compounds: Can accommodate small atoms in crystal voids

Tips to Solve

  • Remember the key definition: partially filled d-orbitals.
  • d-electrons are responsible for most characteristic properties.
  • Multiple oxidation states arise from similar energies of (n-1)d and ns electrons.
  • Color comes from d-d electronic transitions.
  • Magnetic properties depend on unpaired electrons.
  • Catalytic activity is due to ability to change oxidation states.
