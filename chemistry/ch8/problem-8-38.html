d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.38

Original Question

What can be inferred from the magnetic moment values of the complex species listed in question 8.36?

Background Concept — μ, Unpaired Electrons, High-spin Aqua Ions

The spin-only magnetic moment μ (in BM) is approximated by μ = √[n(n+2)], where n is the number of unpaired electrons. For hydrated octahedral 3d ions with H₂O (weak field), high-spin configurations are typical. Thus μ directly reflects the d-electron count and distribution among t₂g and e_g. Deviations from predicted μ suggest stronger ligand fields (low-spin), spin–orbit contributions, or structural distortions (e.g., Jahn–Teller for d⁹).

Answer

  • μ values consistent with high-spin occupancies: Mn²⁺ (d⁵) ≈ 5.92 BM; Fe²⁺ (HS d⁶) ≈ 4.90 BM; Co²⁺ (HS d⁷) ≈ 3.87 BM; Ni²⁺ (HS d⁸) ≈ 2.83 BM; Cu²⁺ (d⁹) ≈ 1.73 BM.
  • Agreement indicates weak-field ligands and octahedral geometry for aqua ions.
  • Significant deviations imply low-spin states, spin–orbit coupling, or distortions.
