d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.31

Original Question

Use Hund's rule to derive the electronic configuration of Ce³⁺ ion, and calculate its magnetic moment on the basis of 'spin-only' formula.

Background Concept — Ce and Ce³⁺, Hund’s Rule, Spin-only

Cerium (Ce, Z = 58) is a lanthanoid with ground-state configuration often written as [Xe] 4f¹ 5d¹ 6s². On forming Ce³⁺, the two 6s electrons and the 5d electron are removed first, giving a 4f¹ configuration: Ce³⁺ = [Xe] 4f¹. Hund’s rule states that electrons occupy degenerate orbitals singly with parallel spins to maximize multiplicity before pairing. The 4f subshell has seven orbitals; with one electron, it remains unpaired. The “spin-only” magnetic moment for first-row transition ions and many 4f ions is approximated by μ = √[n(n+2)] Bohr magnetons (BM), where n is the number of unpaired electrons. For Ce³⁺ (4f¹), n = 1, so μ ≈ √3 ≈ 1.73 BM. While 4f ions can exhibit significant orbital contributions, the spin-only value provides a first, widely used estimate, sufficient for qualitative comparisons and many problem-solving contexts.

Answer

Ce (Z = 58): [Xe] 4f¹ 5d¹ 6s²
Ce³⁺: remove 6s² and 5d¹ → [Xe] 4f¹
Unpaired electrons n = 1 → μ = √[n(n+2)] = √3 ≈ 1.73 BM (spin-only)

Tips to Solve

  • Remove 6s then 5d before 4f when forming trivalent lanthanoid ions.
  • Apply Hund’s rule to determine unpaired electrons in 4f.
  • Use μ = √[n(n+2)] BM for a quick spin-only estimate.
