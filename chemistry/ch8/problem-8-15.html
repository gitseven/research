d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.15

Original Question

Describe the oxidising action of potassium dichromate and write the ionic equations for its reaction with: (i) iodide (ii) iron(II) solution and (iii) H₂S.

Background Concept — Dichromate as Acidic Oxidant

In acidic solution, dichromate Cr₂O₇²⁻ is a powerful oxidising agent. The relevant reduction half‑reaction is: Cr₂O₇²⁻ + 14H⁺ + 6e⁻ → 2Cr³⁺ + 7H₂O (E° ≈ +1.33 V in strongly acidic medium). Accordingly, it oxidises a wide range of reductants, including I⁻ to I₂, Fe²⁺ to Fe³⁺, and H₂S (or S²⁻) to elemental sulfur. Balancing such reactions proceeds via the half‑reaction method: write oxidation and reduction halves, balance atoms and charge (H⁺/H₂O/e⁻), then combine electrons. The medium matters; in basic solution, permanganate is preferred, while dichromate is most common in acid. Colour changes (orange Cr₂O₇²⁻ to green Cr³⁺) allow visual monitoring. Stoichiometries depend on electron counts of the reductant: 6 electrons per dichromate are accepted, so combinations yield integer coefficients that satisfy overall electron balance.

Answer

Ionic equations in acidic medium:

(i) Cr₂O₇²⁻ + 14H⁺ + 6I⁻ → 2Cr³⁺ + 7H₂O + 3I₂
(ii) Cr₂O₇²⁻ + 14H⁺ + 6Fe²⁺ → 2Cr³⁺ + 7H₂O + 6Fe³⁺
(iii) Cr₂O₇²⁻ + 14H⁺ + 3H₂S → 2Cr³⁺ + 7H₂O + 3S

Tips to Solve

  • Use the standard reduction half: Cr₂O₇²⁻ + 14H⁺ + 6e⁻ → 2Cr³⁺ + 7H₂O.
  • Oxidation halves: 2I⁻ → I₂ + 2e⁻; Fe²⁺ → Fe³⁺ + e⁻; H₂S → S + 2H⁺ + 2e⁻.
  • Scale oxidation half‑reactions to deliver 6 electrons before combining.
