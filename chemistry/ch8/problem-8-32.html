d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.32

Original Question

Name the members of the lanthanoid series which exhibit +4 and +2 oxidation states. Correlate this with their electronic configurations.

Background Concept — Stability of f⁰, f⁷, f¹⁴

Lanthanoids commonly show +3 oxidation state. Deviations to +2 or +4 are rationalized by achieving particularly stable f-electron counts: f⁰, f⁷ (half-filled), and f¹⁴ (filled). Thus, +4 is favored when the M⁴⁺ ion attains f⁰ or f⁷; +2 is favored when M²⁺ attains f⁷ or f¹⁴. Environmental factors (lattice energies, solvation, covalency) also play roles, but configurations guide the mnemonic. Ce⁴⁺ has f⁰ (highly stable, strong oxidant), Tb⁴⁺ approaches half-filled stability in certain lattices, and Pr⁴⁺ can be stabilized in oxides. For +2, Eu²⁺ (f⁷) and Yb²⁺ (f¹⁴) are classic, while Sm²⁺ (f⁶) and others may be stabilized under specific conditions. These preferences echo d-block patterns where half-filled and filled subshells confer extra stability.

Answer

+4 state (examples): Ce⁴⁺ (f⁰), Pr⁴⁺ (≈f¹), Tb⁴⁺ (≈f⁷ context-dependent).

+2 state (examples): Eu²⁺ (f⁷), Yb²⁺ (f¹⁴), Sm²⁺ (f⁶; less common but known).

Ce: [Xe] 4f¹ 5d¹ 6s² → Ce⁴⁺: [Xe] (f⁰)
Eu: [Xe] 4f⁷ 6s² → Eu²⁺: [Xe] 4f⁷ (half‑filled)
Yb: [Xe] 4f¹⁴ 6s² → Yb²⁺: [Xe] 4f¹⁴ (filled)

Tips to Solve

  • Use stability of f⁰, f⁷, f¹⁴ to predict unusual +2 or +4 states.
  • List at least one example each for +4 and +2 and show the f-count.
  • Note: Solid-state lattices can stabilize borderline cases (Pr⁴⁺, Tb⁴⁺).
