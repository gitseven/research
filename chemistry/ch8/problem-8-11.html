d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.11

Original Question

Explain giving reasons: (i) Transition metals and many of their compounds show paramagnetic behaviour. (ii) The enthalpies of atomisation of the transition metals are high. (iii) The transition metals generally form coloured compounds. (iv) Transition metals and their many compounds act as good catalyst.

Background Concept — d Electrons and Metallic Bonding

(i) Paramagnetism: unpaired d electrons contribute spin magnetic moments; χ ∝ number of unpaired electrons. (ii) High atomisation enthalpy: strong metallic bonding via delocalised d and s electrons increases cohesive energy. (iii) Colour: d–d transitions in ligand fields and charge‑transfer bands absorb visible light; colour varies with ligand strength and geometry. (iv) Catalysis: multiple accessible oxidation states and vacant/filled d orbitals enable adsorption, bond activation, and redox cycles on surfaces/complexes.

Answer

  • (i) Unpaired d electrons → paramagnetism.
  • (ii) Strong metallic bonding (s/d overlap) → high atomisation enthalpy.
  • (iii) Ligand‑field d–d and charge‑transfer transitions → colour.
  • (iv) Variable valence and adsorption sites → catalytic activity.

Tips to Solve

  • Relate each property directly to d orbitals (occupancy, transitions, bonding).
  • Mention ligand field for colour; surface adsorption for catalysis.
