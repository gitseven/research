d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.28

Original Question

What are inner transition elements? Decide which of the following atomic numbers are the atomic numbers of the inner transition elements: 29, 59, 74, 95, 102, 104.

Background Concept — f-block Classification

Inner transition elements are the f‑block elements consisting of the lanthanoids (Z = 57–71) with filling of 4f orbitals and the actinoids (Z = 89–103) with filling of 5f orbitals. Their chemistry features variable oxidation states, contraction trends (lanthanoid contraction), and significant magnetic and spectroscopic properties due to f‑electrons. Identification by atomic number requires recognizing whether Z lies within 57–71 or 89–103. Numbers outside these ranges correspond to d‑block (transition) or p‑/s‑block elements or superheavy elements beyond the actinoid series.

Answer

Inner transition elements: f‑block (lanthanoids 57–71, actinoids 89–103).

  • 29 (Cu): Not inner transition
  • 59 (Pr): Inner transition (lanthanoid)
  • 74 (W): Not inner transition
  • 95 (Am): Inner transition (actinoid)
  • 102 (No): Inner transition (actinoid)
  • 104 (Rf): Not inner transition (transition metal, group 4)

Tips to Solve

  • Memorize Z ranges: 57–71 and 89–103.
  • Check each atomic number against these intervals.
  • Recall symbols: Pr (59), Am (95), No (102) are classic f‑block examples.
