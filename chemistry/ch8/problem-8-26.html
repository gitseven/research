d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.26

Original Question

Indicate the steps in the preparation of: (i) K₂Cr₂O₇ from chromite ore. (ii) KMnO₄ from pyrolusite ore.

Background Concept — Industrial Preparation Routes

Potassium dichromate and potassium permanganate are prepared industrially from their ores (chromite FeCr₂O₄ and pyrolusite MnO₂) using oxidation and selective precipitation. For K₂Cr₂O₇, chromite is roasted with Na₂CO₃ and air to form Na₂CrO₄, then leached, acidified to dichromate, and finally converted to K₂Cr₂O₇ with KCl due to lower solubility. For KMnO₄, MnO₂ is fused with KOH and oxidant (air/ClO₃⁻) to K₂MnO₄, then oxidized or disproportionated to KMnO₄. These routes exploit thermodynamic stability of high-valent oxo anions and solubility differences between sodium and potassium salts.

Answer

(i) K₂Cr₂O₇ from Chromite:

  • Roast: 4FeCr₂O₄ + 8Na₂CO₃ + 7O₂ → 8Na₂CrO₄ + 2Fe₂O₃ + 8CO₂
  • Leach: Na₂CrO₄ dissolves in water; filter
  • Acidify: 2Na₂CrO₄ + H₂SO₄ → Na₂Cr₂O₇ + Na₂SO₄ + H₂O
  • Precipitate: Na₂Cr₂O₇ + 2KCl → K₂Cr₂O₇↓ + 2NaCl

(ii) KMnO₄ from Pyrolusite:

  • Fuse: 2MnO₂ + 4KOH + O₂ → 2K₂MnO₄ + 2H₂O
  • Oxidize: 2K₂MnO₄ + 2H₂O → 2KMnO₄ + 2KOH + H₂ (electrolytic) or 3K₂MnO₄ + 2H₂O → 2KMnO₄ + MnO₂ + 4KOH
  • Crystallize KMnO₄ (deep purple) on cooling/evaporation

Tips to Solve

  • Memorize sequence keywords: roast → leach → acidify → precipitate (dichromate).
  • For permanganate: fuse → oxidize → crystallize.
  • Potassium salts are less soluble than sodium for dichromate, aiding isolation.
