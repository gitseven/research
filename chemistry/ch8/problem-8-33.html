d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.33

Original Question

Compare the chemistry of actinoids with that of lanthanoids with reference to: (i) Electronic configuration (ii) Oxidation states (iii) Chemical reactivity.

Background Concept — 4f vs 5f Chemistry

Lanthanoids (4f) and actinoids (5f) both involve filling of f-orbitals, but their chemistry diverges because 4f orbitals are core-like and poorly overlap with ligand orbitals, while 5f orbitals are more radially extended and participate in bonding. Consequently, lanthanoids display comparatively uniform chemistry dominated by +3 oxidation state (with limited +2/+4), gradual lanthanoid contraction, and largely ionic bonding. Actinoids exhibit wider oxidation-state ranges (+3 to +7), greater covalency, and more complex redox behavior. Reactivity patterns reflect these differences, with actinoids forming multiple oxo species (e.g., uranyl) and variable coordination geometries. Radiological aspects and limited availability also influence the characterization of actinoid chemistry.

Answer

  • Electronic configuration: Ln: filling 4f; An: filling 5f (with 6d/7s close in energy). 4f less bonding participation; 5f more covalent.
  • Oxidation states: Ln: mainly +3; occasional +2/+4 (Ce⁴⁺, Eu²⁺, Yb²⁺). An: broader range +3 to +7 (U, Np, Pu).
  • Chemical reactivity: Ln: relatively uniform, ionic compounds, slow hydrolysis. An: variable, greater redox diversity, significant oxo-complexes (e.g., UO₂²⁺), stronger ligand field interactions.

Tips to Solve

  • Anchor on 4f (core-like) vs 5f (bonding) to explain most differences.
  • List typical oxidation states and at least one example ion for each series.
  • Mention covalency and oxo-species for actinoids.
