d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.17

Original Question

How is potassium dichromate prepared from chromite ore? Write the chemical equations involved.

Background Concept — Chromite Ore Processing

Chromite ore (FeCr₂O₄) is the primary source of chromium compounds. The preparation of potassium dichromate involves several key steps: roasting with alkali, leaching, acidification, and crystallization. The process begins with roasting chromite ore with sodium carbonate and lime in the presence of air at high temperatures (1000-1100°C). This converts chromium(III) to chromium(VI) in the form of sodium chromate (Na₂CrO₄). The roasted mass is then leached with water to extract the soluble chromate. The solution is filtered and acidified with sulfuric acid to convert chromate to dichromate. Finally, potassium chloride is added to precipitate potassium dichromate, which is less soluble than sodium dichromate. This process is industrially important as K₂Cr₂O₇ is widely used as an oxidizing agent, in leather tanning, and in analytical chemistry. The conversion from chromate to dichromate occurs due to the equilibrium: 2CrO₄²⁻ + 2H⁺ ⇌ Cr₂O₇²⁻ + H₂O, which shifts right in acidic conditions.

Answer

Preparation steps with chemical equations:

1. Roasting: 4FeCr₂O₄ + 8Na₂CO₃ + 7O₂ → 8Na₂CrO₄ + 2Fe₂O₃ + 8CO₂
2. Leaching: Na₂CrO₄ (s) + H₂O → Na₂CrO₄ (aq)
3. Acidification: 2Na₂CrO₄ + H₂SO₄ → Na₂Cr₂O₇ + Na₂SO₄ + H₂O
4. Precipitation: Na₂Cr₂O₇ + 2KCl → K₂Cr₂O₇ + 2NaCl

Tips to Solve

  • Remember the key steps: roast, leach, acidify, precipitate.
  • Roasting converts Cr³⁺ to Cr⁶⁺ in the presence of oxygen and alkali.
  • Acidification converts chromate (CrO₄²⁻) to dichromate (Cr₂O₇²⁻).
  • K₂Cr₂O₇ is less soluble than Na₂Cr₂O₇, enabling precipitation.
