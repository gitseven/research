d- and f-Block Elements · Problem 8.18

Original Question

How is potassium permanganate prepared from pyrolusite ore? Write the chemical equations involved.

Background Concept — Pyrolusite Ore Processing

Pyrolusite (MnO₂) is the most important ore of manganese and serves as the starting material for preparing potassium permanganate. The process involves several key steps: fusion with alkali, oxidation, and electrolytic oxidation. First, pyrolusite is fused with potassium hydroxide and an oxidizing agent (like potassium chlorate or air) at high temperatures to form potassium manganate (K₂MnO₄), which contains manganese in the +6 oxidation state. The green manganate solution is then subjected to electrolytic oxidation or chemical oxidation to convert MnO₄²⁻ to MnO₄⁻. This step is crucial because manganate disproportionates in neutral or acidic solution to form permanganate and manganese dioxide. The final product, KMnO₄, crystallizes out as deep purple crystals. This process is industrially significant as KMnO₄ is a versatile oxidizing agent used in water treatment, organic synthesis, and analytical chemistry. The key challenge is maintaining the +7 oxidation state of manganese, which is highly oxidizing and tends to reduce back to lower oxidation states.

Answer

Preparation steps with chemical equations:

1. Fusion: 2MnO₂ + 4KOH + O₂ → 2K₂MnO₄ + 2H₂O
2. Electrolytic oxidation: 2K₂MnO₄ + 2H₂O → 2KMnO₄ + 2KOH + H₂
Alternative chemical oxidation: 3K₂MnO₄ + 2H₂O → 2KMnO₄ + MnO₂ + 4KOH

Tips to Solve

  • Pyrolusite contains MnO₂ (Mn⁴⁺) which needs to be oxidized to Mn⁷⁺.
  • Fusion with KOH and O₂ produces green K₂MnO₄ (Mn⁶⁺).
  • Electrolytic oxidation is preferred over chemical oxidation for better yield.
  • Manganate disproportionates in neutral/acidic solution: 3MnO₄²⁻ + 4H⁺ → 2MnO₄⁻ + MnO₂ + 2H₂O.
