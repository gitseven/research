Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.25

Original Question

Sucrose decomposes in acid solution into glucose and fructose according to the first order rate law, with t₁/₂ = 3.00 hours. What fraction of sample of sucrose remains after 8 hours?

Background Concept — Using Half-life to Get Fraction

For first-order reactions, the fraction remaining after time t is f = e^(−kt) with k = 0.693/t₁/₂. Combining gives f = e^{−(0.693 t / t₁/₂)} = 2^{−(t/t₁/₂)}. Thus, one can compute the fraction by counting half-lives: after n half-lives, f = (1/2)^n. For non-integer multiples, the exponential form provides an exact value.

Answer

t/t₁/₂ = 8 / 3.00 = 2.6667 → f = 2^{−2.6667} = 0.157 (≈ 15.7%)

Tips to Solve

  • Use f = 2^{−(t/t₁/₂)} for quick half-life calculations.
  • Alternatively, compute k then f = e^(−kt) to the same result.
  • Check: 3 half-lives would give 12.5%; at 2.67 half-lives, 15–16% is reasonable.
