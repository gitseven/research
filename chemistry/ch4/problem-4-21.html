Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.21

Original Question

The following data were obtained during the first order thermal decomposition of SO₂Cl₂ at a constant volume. SO₂Cl₂ (g) → SO₂ (g) + Cl₂ (g). Calculate the rate of the reaction when total pressure is 0.65 atm.

Background Concept — Rate from Pressure at a Given Time

For SO₂Cl₂ → SO₂ + Cl₂ in a constant-volume system, each mole of reactant forms two moles of products, increasing total moles and total pressure from P₀ to P(t) to P∞ as decomposition proceeds. If the reaction is first order in SO₂Cl₂, the extent can be inferred from pressure: define α(t) as fraction decomposed. Then P(t) = P₀(1 + α), and the instantaneous rate r = −d[SO₂Cl₂]/dt = k[SO₂Cl₂] = k·(n/V) = k·(P₀/RT)·(1 − α), where α = (P(t) − P₀)/P₀. Given P(t), P₀, T, and V (or using RT relations), one can compute α and hence r. Without explicit P₀ and T, the rate can be expressed in pressure units per time via dP/dt relationships derived from first‑order decay: d(P − P₀)/dt = k(P∞ − P(t)).

Answer

Compute α at P = 0.65 atm: α = (P − P₀)/P₀. Then rate r = k·(P₀/RT)(1 − α) in mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹, or use r = k(P∞ − P) in pressure/time units if k is known from earlier data.

r = k(P∞ − P) (constant V,T), or r = k[SO₂Cl₂] = k·(P₀/RT)(1 − α)

Tips to Solve

  • First find k from the provided dataset (e.g., slope of ln(P∞ − Pₜ) vs t), then evaluate r at P = 0.65 atm.
  • Ensure units: if you report rate in mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹, include RT and volume; for pressure/time, use atm s⁻¹.
  • Use P∞ = P₀(1 + 1) = 2P₀ at full conversion for this stoichiometry in a closed vessel.
