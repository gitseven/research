Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.28

Original Question

The decomposition of A into product has value of k as 4.5 × 10⁻³ s⁻¹ at 10°C and energy of activation 60 kJ mol⁻¹. At what temperature would k be 1.5 × 10⁻² s⁻¹?

Background Concept — Two-Point Arrhenius Temperature Solve

With Eₐ known, the Arrhenius ratio form ln(k₂/k₁) = −Eₐ/R (1/T₂ − 1/T₁) allows solving for the unknown temperature T₂. Convert temperatures to kelvin and Eₐ to J mol⁻¹. Because ln(k₂/k₁) is positive when k₂ > k₁, the right-hand side must also be positive; since Eₐ/R is positive, this implies (1/T₂ − 1/T₁) is negative (i.e., T₂ > T₁), as expected.

Answer

ln(1.5e−2 / 4.5e−3) = ln(3.333) = 1.20397
1/T₂ = 1/T₁ − (R/Eₐ) ln(k₂/k₁) = 1/283.15 − (8.314/60000)×1.20397
1/T₂ = 0.003532 − 0.0001668 = 0.003365 → T₂ ≈ 297 K (≈ 24 °C)

Tips to Solve

  • Always convert °C to K and k ratio inside a natural logarithm.
  • Check sign: T₂ should be higher when k₂ > k₁ for positive Eₐ.
  • Keep Eₐ in J mol⁻¹ if R is in J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹.
