Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.20

Original Question

For the decomposition of azoisopropane to hexane and nitrogen at 543 K, the following data are obtained. Calculate the rate constant.

Background Concept — First-order from Total Pressure Rise

Gas‑phase decompositions that increase the number of moles cause the total pressure to rise in a closed, constant‑volume system. For a first‑order reaction A → products with stoichiometry producing Δν additional moles per mole of A, the total pressure evolves from P₀ at t = 0 to P∞ at completion. The extent of reaction x relates partial pressures to P(t). A standard result is that ln(P∞ − P₀) − ln(P∞ − P(t)) = kt, or equivalently ln[(P∞ − Pₜ)] = ln(P∞ − P₀) − kt. Thus, plotting ln(P∞ − Pₜ) versus t gives a straight line of slope −k. If P∞ is not measured, it can be inferred from the stoichiometry and the limiting pressure at long times. Using any two time points, k can be computed via k = (1/Δt) ln[(P∞ − Pₜ₁)/(P∞ − Pₜ₂)].

Answer

Using the provided pressure–time data (not shown), compute P∞ from the asymptote, then evaluate k from the slope of ln(P∞ − Pₜ) vs t or using a pair of points.

k = (1/Δt) ln[(P∞ − P₁)/(P∞ − P₂)] (units: s⁻¹ if t in s)

Tips to Solve

  • Ensure constant volume and temperature; otherwise, pressure changes are not purely kinetic.
  • Use late‑time data to estimate P∞ reliably, or fit all data to extract both P∞ and k.
  • Check linearity of ln(P∞ − Pₜ) vs t as a diagnostic of first order.
