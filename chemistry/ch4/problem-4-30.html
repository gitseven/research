Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.30

Original Question

The rate of a reaction quadruples when the temperature changes from 293 K to 313 K. Calculate the energy of activation of the reaction assuming that it does not change with temperature.

Background Concept — Two-Point Arrhenius with Rate Ratio

Using k₂/k₁ = exp[Eₐ/R (1/T₁ − 1/T₂)], one solves for Eₐ directly from the observed factor change with temperature. When k₂/k₁ = 4, ln 4 multiplies the reciprocal temperature difference scaled by R. The method assumes constant Eₐ over the interval, which is typical for moderate temperature ranges.

Answer

Eₐ = R ln 4 / (1/293 − 1/313) = 8.314×1.386 / (0.003413 − 0.003194) ≈ 5.27×10⁴ J mol⁻¹ ≈ 52.7 kJ mol⁻¹

Tips to Solve

  • Use natural log of the rate ratio; ensure temperatures are in kelvin.
  • Check sign consistency: denominator (1/T₁ − 1/T₂) must be positive if T₂ > T₁ placed in that order.
  • Report Eₐ in kJ mol⁻¹ for clarity.
