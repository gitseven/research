Chapter 4 · Problem 4

Question

The rate constant of a reaction at 300 K is 2.0 × 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹ and at 320 K is 8.0 × 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹. Calculate the activation energy.

Background Concept — Arrhenius Equation

The Arrhenius equation relates rate constant to temperature: k = A e^(-Ea/RT), where A is pre-exponential factor, Ea is activation energy, R is gas constant, and T is temperature. The logarithmic form is ln(k₂/k₁) = (Ea/R)(1/T₁ - 1/T₂). Activation energy is the minimum energy required for reactants to form products.

Visualization

Solution

Using Arrhenius equation: ln(k₂/k₁) = (Ea/R)(1/T₁ - 1/T₂). Given k₁ = 2.0 × 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹ at T₁ = 300 K, k₂ = 8.0 × 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹ at T₂ = 320 K. ln(8.0 × 10⁻⁵/2.0 × 10⁻⁵) = (Ea/8.314)(1/300 - 1/320). ln(4) = (Ea/8.314)(0.00333 - 0.00313). 1.386 = (Ea/8.314)(0.0002). Ea = (1.386 × 8.314)/0.0002 = 57,600 J/mol = 57.6 kJ/mol.

Tips to Solve

  • Use ln(k₂/k₁) = (Ea/R)(1/T₁ - 1/T₂)
  • Convert temperatures to Kelvin
  • Calculate the ratio k₂/k₁
  • Solve for Ea
  • Convert to kJ/mol

Key Formulas

Arrhenius: ln(k₂/k₁) = (Ea/R)(1/T₁ - 1/T₂); k = A e^(-Ea/RT)
