Chapter 4 · Problem 1

Question

For the reaction 2A + B → 3C, the rate of disappearance of A is 0.1 mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹. Calculate the rate of appearance of C.

Background Concept — Rate of Reaction

The rate of a chemical reaction is the change in concentration of reactants or products per unit time. For the reaction aA + bB → cC + dD, the rate is related by: Rate = -(1/a)(d[A]/dt) = -(1/b)(d[B]/dt) = (1/c)(d[C]/dt) = (1/d)(d[D]/dt). The negative sign indicates decrease in reactant concentration.

Visualization

Solution

For the reaction 2A + B → 3C: Rate = -(1/2)(d[A]/dt) = (1/3)(d[C]/dt). Given d[A]/dt = -0.1 mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹. Therefore: (1/3)(d[C]/dt) = -(1/2)(-0.1) = 0.05. So d[C]/dt = 0.05 × 3 = 0.15 mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹. The rate of appearance of C is 0.15 mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹.

Tips to Solve

  • Use the stoichiometric relationship
  • Apply Rate = (1/coefficient)(d[concentration]/dt)
  • Watch signs for reactants vs products
  • Check units consistency
  • Verify with stoichiometry

Key Formulas

Rate = -(1/a)(d[A]/dt) = (1/c)(d[C]/dt); For 2A + B → 3C: Rate = -(1/2)(d[A]/dt) = (1/3)(d[C]/dt)
