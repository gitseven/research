Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.19

Original Question

A first order reaction takes 40 min for 30% decomposition. Calculate t₁/₂.

Background Concept — Using a Known Conversion to Find k

For first‑order decay, ln([A]₀/[A]) = kt. If a fraction x has decomposed in time t, then [A] = (1−x)[A]₀ and ln(1/(1−x)) = kt. From this, the rate constant is k = (1/t) ln(1/(1−x)). Once k is known, the half‑life follows from t₁/₂ = 0.693/k. This two‑step procedure is standard when a non‑50% conversion at a specified time is given: compute k from the conversion, then compute t₁/₂.

Answer

x = 0.30; t = 40 min → k = (1/40) ln(1/0.70) = (1/40) × 0.356675 = 8.917×10⁻³ min⁻¹
t₁/₂ = 0.693/k = 0.693 / 8.917×10⁻³ = 77.7 min

Tips to Solve

  • Compute k from the given conversion, then use t₁/₂ = 0.693/k.
  • Keep consistent time units (minutes here) throughout.
  • Sanity check: less than one half‑life to reach 30% conversion.
