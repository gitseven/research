Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.24

Original Question

Consider a certain reaction A → Products with k = 2.0 × 10⁻² s⁻¹. Calculate the concentration of A remaining after 100 s if the initial concentration of A is 1.0 mol L⁻¹.

Background Concept — Integrated First-Order Law

For first-order decay, [A](t) = [A]₀ e^(−kt). The exponential factor e^(−kt) gives the fraction remaining after time t. Because k is in s⁻¹ here, t must be in seconds. This simple form allows direct evaluation of remaining concentration without intermediate steps.

Answer

[A](100 s) = 1.0 × e^(−0.02 × 100) = e^(−2) = 0.135 mol L⁻¹

Tips to Solve

  • Use [A] = [A]₀ e^(−kt); ensure k and t are in reciprocal units.
  • e^(−2) ≈ 0.1353 is a useful constant to remember.
  • If asked for fraction remaining, report e^(−kt) directly.
