Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.27

Original Question

The rate constant for the first order decomposition of H₂O₂ is given by: log k = 14.34 − 1.25 × 10⁴ K/T. Calculate Eₐ for this reaction and at what temperature will its half-period be 256 minutes?

Background Concept — Base-10 Arrhenius Form

Arrhenius in base‑10 logarithms reads: log k = log A − Eₐ/(2.303 RT). Comparing with log k = a − b/T, one identifies b = Eₐ/(2.303 R) and a = log A. Thus Eₐ = b(2.303R). After extracting Eₐ, the temperature for a specified half‑period follows from k = 0.693/t₁/₂ and k(T) = 10^{a − b/T}. Solve T from log k = a − b/T → T = b/(a − log k). Ensure time units for k (s⁻¹ or min⁻¹) match the half‑period units.

Answer

Eₐ = 2.303 R b = 2.303 × 8.314 × 1.25×10⁴ = 2.39×10⁵ J mol⁻¹ ≈ 239 kJ mol⁻¹
t₁/₂ = 256 min → k = 0.693/256 = 2.707×10⁻³ min⁻¹ → log k = −2.567
T = b/(a − log k) = 1.25×10⁴ / (14.34 − (−2.567)) = 1.25×10⁴ / 16.907 ≈ 739 K

(If k is desired in s⁻¹, convert minutes to seconds consistently before solving.)

Tips to Solve

  • Use base‑10 identity: Eₐ = 2.303 R b when log k = a − b/T.
  • Compute k from half‑period first, then solve for T.
  • Keep unit consistency (min⁻¹ vs s⁻¹) throughout.
