Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.29

Original Question

The time required for 10% completion of a first order reaction at 298 K is equal to that required for its 25% completion at 308 K. If A = 4 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹, calculate k at 318 K and Eₐ.

Background Concept — Combining Fractional Times with Arrhenius

For first order, the time for fractional completion x is t(x) = (2.303/k) log(1/(1−x)). Equality of times at two temperatures implies (2.303/k₁) log[1/(1−x₁)] = (2.303/k₂) log[1/(1−x₂)], giving a ratio of rate constants k₂/k₁ = log[1/(1−x₂)] / log[1/(1−x₁)]. With k₂/k₁ known, Arrhenius ln(k₂/k₁) = −Eₐ/R (1/T₂ − 1/T₁) yields Eₐ. Finally, k at a third temperature follows from k = A e^(−Eₐ/RT).

Answer

x₁ = 0.10 @ 298 K; x₂ = 0.25 @ 308 K
k₂/k₁ = ln(1/0.75)/ln(1/0.90) = 0.28768 / 0.10536 = 2.731
Eₐ = R ln(k₂/k₁) / (1/T₁ − 1/T₂) = 8.314×1.005 / (1/298 − 1/308) ≈ 7.67×10⁴ J mol⁻¹ ≈ 76.7 kJ mol⁻¹
k(318 K) = A e^{−Eₐ/(RT)} = 4×10¹⁰ × e^{−7.67×10⁴/(8.314×318)} ≈ 1.02×10⁻² s⁻¹

Tips to Solve

  • Use natural logs for the time ratio; the base cancels if consistent.
  • Compute Eₐ from the 298–308 K pair; then apply A, Eₐ to 318 K.
  • Check that k increases with T and that Eₐ has units of kJ mol⁻¹.
