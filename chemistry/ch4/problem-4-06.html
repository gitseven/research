Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.6

Original Question

Time required to decompose SO₂Cl₂ to half of its initial amount is 60 minutes. If the decomposition is a first order reaction, calculate the rate constant of the reaction.

Background Concept — First-Order Half-Life

First-order reactions display exponential decay of reactant concentration with time: [A](t) = [A]₀ e^(−kt). A consequence is that the time to reduce the amount by any fixed fraction depends only on the rate constant k and not on the initial concentration. The half‑life t₁/₂ is obtained by setting [A](t₁/₂) = [A]₀/2, giving 1/2 = e^(−kt₁/₂) and hence t₁/₂ = (ln 2)/k. This property allows quick determination of k from measured half-lives and, conversely, prediction of how many half-lives correspond to a specified conversion. Because many processes (radioactive decay, unimolecular decompositions, pharmacokinetic elimination) follow first-order kinetics, t₁/₂ is a widely reported and intuitively accessible metric. Ensure unit consistency: if t₁/₂ is in minutes, k will be in min⁻¹; conversions to s⁻¹ or h⁻¹ can be made afterward. When data are available at multiple temperatures, Arrhenius analysis (ln k vs 1/T) reveals activation energy and pre-exponential factors, but for a single measurement, the half-life relation is the most direct route to k.

Answer

k = (ln 2)/t₁/₂ = 0.693 / 60 min = 1.155×10⁻² min⁻¹

In SI: k = 1.155×10⁻² / 60 = 1.925×10⁻⁴ s⁻¹.

Tips to Solve

  • For first order, t₁/₂ = 0.693/k; for zero and second order, half-life depends on [A]₀.
  • Keep the units of k consistent with the reported t₁/₂ (minutes vs seconds).
  • Check reasonableness: shorter half-life implies larger k.
