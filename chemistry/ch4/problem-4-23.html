Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.23

Original Question

The rate constant for the decomposition of hydrocarbons is 2.418 × 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹ at 546 K. If the energy of activation is 179.9 kJ mol⁻¹, what will be the value of pre‑exponential factor?

Background Concept — Solving A from Arrhenius

Given k, T, and Eₐ, the Arrhenius equation can be rearranged to A = k e^(Eₐ/RT). Using Eₐ in J mol⁻¹ and R = 8.314 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹ ensures consistent units. For unimolecular first‑order decompositions, typical A values range from 10¹² to 10¹⁵ s⁻¹, reflecting vibrational frequencies and steric factors. If a computed A falls grossly outside this range, reassess unit conversions, particularly the energy units and temperature.

Answer

A = k e^(Eₐ/RT) = 2.418×10⁻⁵ × exp(1.799×10⁵ / (8.314×546)) ≈ 2.418×10⁻⁵ × e^{39.65} ≈ 3.7×10¹² s⁻¹

Tips to Solve

  • Convert Eₐ to J mol⁻¹; use kelvin for T.
  • Check that A has units of s⁻¹ for first‑order reactions.
  • Sanity check: A typically ~10¹²–10¹⁵ s⁻¹ for unimolecular gas reactions.
