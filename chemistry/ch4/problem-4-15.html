Chemical Kinetics · Problem 4.15

Original Question

The experimental data for decomposition of N₂O₅ [2N₂O₅ → 4NO₂ + O₂] in gas phase at 318K are given below: (i) Plot [N₂O₅] against t. (ii) Find the half-life period for the reaction. (iii) Draw a graph between log[N₂O₅] and t. (iv) What is the rate law?

Background Concept — Diagnostic Plots for Order

Identifying reaction order from concentration–time data relies on integrated forms and their linear plots. For zero order, [A] vs t is linear with slope −k; for first order, ln[A] vs t (or log₁₀[A] vs t) is linear with slope −k (or −k/2.303); for second order (single reactant), 1/[A] vs t is linear with slope +k. Constructing these plots from the data and assessing linearity (R², residuals) reveals the order and gives k from the slope. The half-life further supports the diagnosis: for first order, t₁/₂ = 0.693/k is constant and independent of [A]₀; for zero and second order, t₁/₂ depends on [A]₀. For the thermal decomposition of N₂O₅ in the gas phase, first-order kinetics are commonly observed under conditions where decomposition proceeds via a unimolecular step or a steady-state mechanism that yields an apparent first-order rate law. Thus, log plots should yield straight lines and the slope magnitude gives k/2.303 for base‑10 logs.

Answer

(i) Plot [N₂O₅] vs t: decaying curve. (ii) Determine t where [N₂O₅] halves from its initial value (constant across intervals if first order). (iii) Graph log[N₂O₅] vs t is linear; slope = −k/2.303. (iv) Rate law: first order, r = k[N₂O₅].

Tips to Solve

  • Use log₁₀ for convenience; convert slope to k via k = −2.303 × slope.
  • Check if half-lives from the data are roughly constant to confirm first order.
  • For noisy data, fit a straight line to log plot and report R².
