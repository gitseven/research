Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.31

Original Question

The correct order of wavelengths of absorption in the visible region for the complexes [Ni(NO₂)₆]⁴⁻, [Ni(NH₃)₆]²⁺ and [Ni(H₂O)₆]²⁺ is: (i) [Ni(NO₂)₆]⁴⁻ < [Ni(NH₃)₆]²⁺ < [Ni(H₂O)₆]²⁺ (ii) [Ni(NO₂)₆]⁴⁻ < [Ni(H₂O)₆]²⁺ < [Ni(NH₃)₆]²⁺ (iii) [Ni(H₂O)₆]²⁺ < [Ni(NH₃)₆]²⁺ < [Ni(NO₂)₆]⁴⁻ (iv) [Ni(NH₃)₆]²⁺ < [Ni(H₂O)₆]²⁺ < [Ni(NO₂)₆]⁴⁻

Background Concept — Ligand Field Strength vs Absorption Wavelength

For octahedral d–d transitions, the absorbed photon energy approximately equals the crystal field splitting Δ₀. Strong‑field ligands produce larger Δ₀ and therefore require higher energy (shorter wavelength) light for excitation; weak‑field ligands yield smaller Δ₀ and absorb lower energy (longer wavelength) light. The spectrochemical series relevant here places NO₂⁻ (as nitro‑N) stronger than NH₃, which is stronger than H₂O: H₂O < NH₃ < NO₂⁻ (increasing field strength). Thus the absorption wavelength order (from shortest to longest) is NO₂⁻ < NH₃ < H₂O; equivalently, from longest to shortest it is H₂O > NH₃ > NO₂⁻. Matching to options given, the increasing order of absorption wavelengths is [Ni(NO₂)₆]⁴⁻ < [Ni(NH₃)₆]²⁺ < [Ni(H₂O)₆]²⁺.

Answer

Correct option: (i) [Ni(NO₂)₆]⁴⁻ < [Ni(NH₃)₆]²⁺ < [Ni(H₂O)₆]²⁺

  • Reason: NO₂⁻ (strongest) → shortest λ; H₂O (weakest) → longest λ

Tips to Solve

  • Map ligands onto spectrochemical series: H₂O < NH₃ < NO₂⁻.
  • Stronger field → larger Δ₀ → higher energy → shorter wavelength.
