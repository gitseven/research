Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.15

Original Question

Discuss the nature of bonding in the following coordination entities on the basis of valence bond theory: (i) [Fe(CN)₆]⁴⁻ (ii) [FeF₆]³⁻ (iii) [Co(C₂O₄)₃]³⁻ (iv) [CoF₆]³⁻

Background Concept — VBT: Inner- vs Outer-Orbital Complexes

Valence Bond Theory (VBT) describes metal–ligand bonds as coordinate covalent interactions formed by overlap of ligand lone pairs with vacant hybrid orbitals on the metal. The hybrid set depends on coordination number and whether d-orbitals from the inner (n−1)d or outer nd shell are used. For octahedral complexes, inner‑orbital (d²sp³) hybrids yield low‑spin complexes with strong‑field ligands that pair d electrons, whereas outer‑orbital (sp³d²) hybrids correspond to high‑spin systems with weak‑field ligands and minimal pairing. While VBT rationalizes spin states and magnetic moments, Crystal Field and MO theories better explain spectra. Still, VBT classification (inner/outer) remains a common exam approach for quick predictions using ligand strength (e.g., CN⁻ strong; F⁻ weak; oxalate intermediate bidentate).

Answer

  • (i) [Fe(CN)₆]⁴⁻: Fe²⁺ (d⁶), CN⁻ strong‑field → low‑spin, inner‑orbital d²sp³ (octahedral), diamagnetic (t₂g⁶).
  • (ii) [FeF₆]³⁻: Fe³⁺ (d⁵), F⁻ weak‑field → high‑spin, outer‑orbital sp³d² (octahedral), paramagnetic (5 unpaired).
  • (iii) [Co(C₂O₄)₃]³⁻: Co³⁺ (d⁶), oxalate moderately strong and chelating → often low‑spin, inner‑orbital d²sp³; paramagnetism depends on field strength (many are low‑spin, diamagnetic).
  • (iv) [CoF₆]³⁻: Co³⁺ (d⁶), F⁻ weak‑field → high‑spin, outer‑orbital sp³d² (octahedral), paramagnetic (4 unpaired).
Inner‑orbital (d²sp³) vs Outer‑orbital (sp³d²) decided by ligand field strength and electron pairing.

Tips to Solve

  • Determine oxidation state and d‑count first.
  • Use spectrochemical series (CN⁻ strong; F⁻ weak; C₂O₄²⁻ intermediate).
  • Assign inner (low‑spin) or outer (high‑spin) hybridization accordingly.
