Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.29

Original Question

The complex with the highest magnetic moment value is: (i) [Cr(H₂O)₆]³⁺ (ii) [Fe(H₂O)₆]³⁺ (iii) [Zn(H₂O)₆]²⁺

Background Concept — Spin-Only Magnetic Moments

Magnetic moment (spin-only) is μ ≈ √[n(n+2)] BM, where n is the number of unpaired electrons. High‑spin hexaaqua ions with weak‑field H₂O ligands typically maximize unpaired electrons for a given d‑count. Cr³⁺ is d³ (three unpaired), Fe³⁺ is d⁵ (five unpaired), Zn²⁺ is d¹⁰ (no unpaired). Therefore, Fe³⁺ has the highest μ.

Answer

Highest μ: [Fe(H₂O)₆]³⁺ (d⁵, n = 5 → μ ≈ √35 ≈ 5.92 BM)
