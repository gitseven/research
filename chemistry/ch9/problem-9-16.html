Original Question

Draw figure to show the splitting of d orbitals in an octahedral crystal field.

Background Concept — Octahedral Crystal Field Splitting

In an octahedral field, six ligands approach along the axes, raising the energy of orbitals that point directly at ligands (dₓ²₋ᵧ² and d_z²) and lowering the energy of those oriented between axes (d_xy, d_xz, d_yz). The five d orbitals thus split into a lower‑energy triplet t₂g (d_xy, d_xz, d_yz) and a higher‑energy doublet e_g (dₓ²₋ᵧ², d_z²). The energy gap is the octahedral crystal field splitting parameter Δ₀. Electron filling across t₂g and e_g depends on Δ₀ vs pairing energy: weak fields give high‑spin configurations; strong fields give low‑spin. This splitting underlies the magnetism and colors of transition‑metal complexes.
