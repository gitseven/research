Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.9

Original Question

How many geometrical isomers are possible for the following coordination entities: (i) [Cr(C₂O₄)₃]³⁻ (ii) [Co(NH₃)₃Cl₃]

Background Concept — Octahedral Geometrical Isomerism

Octahedral complexes exhibit geometrical isomerism depending on ligand types. For [M(AA)₃] with three bidentate ligands (like oxalate or en), only one geometrical form exists (all facially equivalent), but the complex can be optically active (Δ/Λ). For [M(A)₃B₃] with two sets of three monodentate ligands, two geometrical isomers exist: fac (three identical ligands on one face) and mer (arranged meridionally). Each geometrical form may or may not be optically active depending on symmetry.

Answer

  • (i) [Cr(C₂O₄)₃]³⁻: One geometrical form; optically active (Δ/Λ enantiomers).
  • (ii) [Co(NH₃)₃Cl₃]: Two geometrical isomers — fac and mer.

Tips to Solve

  • For (AA)₃ octahedral complexes, think optical isomerism rather than geometrical.
  • For A₃B₃ octahedral, count fac vs mer.
