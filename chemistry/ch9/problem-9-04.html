Question

Explain crystal field splitting in octahedral complexes and calculate CFSE for [Fe(CN)₆]³⁻.

Background Concept — Crystal Field Theory

Crystal field theory explains the electronic structure and properties of coordination compounds. In octahedral complexes, the d orbitals split into two sets: t₂g (lower energy) and eg (higher energy). The energy difference is called crystal field splitting energy (Δo). CFSE = (0.4n₁ - 0.6n₂)Δo, where n₁ is electrons in t₂g and n₂ is electrons in eg.

Visualization

Solution

In octahedral complexes, d orbitals split into t₂g (dxy, dxz, dyz) and eg (dx²-y², dz²). For [Fe(CN)₆]³⁻: Fe³⁺ has d⁵ configuration. In strong field (CN⁻), all 5 electrons pair up in t₂g orbitals: t₂g⁵ eg⁰. CFSE = (0.4×5 - 0.6×0)Δo = 2.0Δo. This is a low-spin complex with maximum CFSE due to strong field ligand CN⁻.

Tips to Solve

  • Identify the d orbital splitting
  • Determine electron configuration
  • Apply CFSE formula
  • Consider field strength of ligand
  • Check for high-spin vs low-spin

Key Formulas

CFSE = (0.4n₁ - 0.6n₂)Δo; t₂g (lower), eg (higher)
