Chapter 9 · Problem 2

Question

Write the IUPAC name of [Co(NH₃)₄Cl₂]Cl and [Pt(NH₃)₂Cl₂].

Background Concept — IUPAC Nomenclature

IUPAC nomenclature of coordination compounds follows specific rules: (1) Cation is named first, then anion, (2) In complex ion, ligands are named in alphabetical order, (3) Ligands ending in -ide become -ido, -ite becomes -ito, -ate becomes -ato, (4) Neutral ligands retain their names, (5) Greek prefixes indicate number of ligands, (6) Metal oxidation state is indicated by Roman numerals in parentheses.

Visualization

Solution

[Co(NH₃)₄Cl₂]Cl: Tetraamminedichloridocobalt(III) chloride. [Pt(NH₃)₂Cl₂]: Diamminedichloridoplatinum(II). In the first compound, Co has oxidation state +3 (4×0 + 2×(-1) + 1×(-1) = +3). In the second compound, Pt has oxidation state +2 (2×0 + 2×(-1) = +2). The ligands are named in alphabetical order: ammine, chlorido.

Tips to Solve

  • Name ligands in alphabetical order
  • Use appropriate endings for ligands
  • Calculate oxidation state of metal
  • Use Greek prefixes for numbers
  • Write in correct order

Key Formulas

Ligands in alphabetical order; Metal oxidation state in Roman numerals
