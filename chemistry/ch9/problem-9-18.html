Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.18

Original Question

What is crystal field splitting energy? How does the magnitude of Δ₀ decide the actual configuration of d orbitals in a coordination entity?

Background Concept — Δ₀ vs Pairing Energy (P)

Crystal Field Theory models the effect of surrounding ligands as an electrostatic field that lifts the degeneracy of metal d orbitals. In an octahedral field, the five d orbitals split into lower‑energy t₂g (d_xy, d_xz, d_yz) and higher‑energy e_g (d_x²−y², d_z²). The energy gap between these sets is the octahedral crystal field splitting energy, Δ₀. Electron distribution is governed by competition between Δ₀ and the intra‑orbital pairing energy P. If Δ₀ is small (weak‑field ligands), it is energetically favorable to place electrons in e_g rather than pair in t₂g, giving high‑spin configurations with more unpaired electrons. If Δ₀ is large (strong‑field ligands), electrons pair in t₂g before occupying e_g, yielding low‑spin configurations with fewer unpaired electrons. Δ₀ depends on ligand type (spectrochemical series), metal identity and oxidation state (higher oxidation → larger Δ₀), and geometry (Δ_t < Δ₀ for tetrahedral). The observed magnetic moment and color follow from the resulting electronic configuration and the energy of d–d transitions.

Answer

  • Crystal field splitting energy (Δ₀): energy gap between t₂g and e_g in octahedral field.
  • If Δ₀ < P: high‑spin (maximize unpaired electrons; occupy e_g before pairing).
  • If Δ₀ > P: low‑spin (pair in t₂g before occupying e_g).
Weak field: I⁻, Br⁻, Cl⁻, F⁻, H₂O → small Δ₀ → high‑spin. Strong field: NH₃, en, NO₂⁻(N), CN⁻, CO → large Δ₀ → low‑spin.
