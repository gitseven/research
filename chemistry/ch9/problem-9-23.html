Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.23

Original Question

Give the oxidation state, d orbital occupation and coordination number of the central metal ion in the following complexes: (i) K₃[Co(C₂O₄)₃] (ii) cis-[CrCl₂(en)₂]Cl (iii) (NH₄)₂[CoF₆] (iv) [Mn(H₂O)₆]SO₄

Background Concept — How to Deduce OS, d Count, CN

To determine oxidation state (OS), sum known ligand charges and equate to the complex ion/compound charge. Oxalate (C₂O₄²⁻) is −2 and bidentate; en is neutral and bidentate; halides are −1; aqua is neutral; fluoride is −1. Once OS is known, compute the metal’s d‑electron count from the neutral atom configuration minus electrons corresponding to the OS. Coordination number (CN) equals the number of donor atoms directly bound (bidentate ligands contribute two). This method works independent of spin state; crystal field considerations affect magnetic properties but not OS/CN.

Answer

(i) K₃[Co(C₂O₄)₃] → complex charge −3; 3×oxalate = −6 ⇒ Co is +3; d count: Co³⁺ = d⁶; CN = 3×2 = 6
(ii) cis-[CrCl₂(en)₂]Cl → inner complex is [CrCl₂(en)₂]⁺ (to pair with Cl⁻); ligands: 2Cl⁻ (−2), 2 en (0) ⇒ Cr is +3; d count: Cr³⁺ = d³; CN = 2 (Cl) + 2×2 (en) = 6
(iii) (NH₄)₂[CoF₆] → complex charge −2; 6F⁻ = −6 ⇒ Co is +4; d count: Co⁴⁺ = d⁵; CN = 6
(iv) [Mn(H₂O)₆]SO₄ → complex cation [Mn(H₂O)₆]²⁺ (balances SO₄²⁻); Mn is +2; d count: Mn²⁺ = d⁵; CN = 6

Tips to Solve

  • Oxalate = −2 and bidentate; en = 0 and bidentate; F⁻/Cl⁻ = −1; H₂O = 0.
  • Find complex ion charge from counter‑ions; then solve for metal OS.
  • CN counts donor sites, not ligand count.
