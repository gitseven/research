Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.12

Original Question

Write all the geometrical isomers of [Pt(NH₃)(Br)(Cl)(py)] and how many of these will exhibit optical isomerism?

Background Concept — Square‑Planar MABCD Isomerism

Platinum(II) typically forms square‑planar complexes. For a square‑planar complex with four different monodentate ligands (MABCD), three distinct geometrical isomers are possible, defined by which ligand is trans to a given reference ligand (e.g., NH₃). Each arrangement gives a unique set of cis/trans relationships among the four ligands. Square‑planar complexes lacking chelation almost always possess symmetry elements that render them achiral; thus optical isomerism is not expected for MABCD in square‑planar geometry. Optical activity generally appears in octahedral chelates ([M(AA)₃] types) or when suitably arranged bidentate ligands remove improper symmetry elements. Therefore, [Pt(NH₃)(Br)(Cl)(py)] should show three geometrical isomers and none optically active.

Answer

  • Number of geometrical isomers: 3 (square‑planar MABCD)
  • Optical isomerism: None (all three are achiral)
