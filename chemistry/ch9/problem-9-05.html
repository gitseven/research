Question

Explain the bonding in coordination compounds using valence bond theory with reference to [Ni(CN)₄]²⁻.

Background Concept — Bonding in Coordination Compounds

Valence bond theory explains bonding in coordination compounds by considering hybridization of metal orbitals. The type of hybridization depends on the coordination number and geometry. For square planar complexes like [Ni(CN)₄]²⁻, the metal uses dsp² hybridization. The hybrid orbitals overlap with ligand orbitals to form coordinate bonds.

Visualization

Solution

For [Ni(CN)₄]²⁻: Ni²⁺ has d⁸ configuration. To form square planar geometry, Ni²⁺ uses dsp² hybridization (one d, one s, two p orbitals). The four hybrid orbitals point towards the corners of a square and overlap with CN⁻ orbitals to form four coordinate bonds. This explains the square planar geometry and the formation of coordinate bonds.

Tips to Solve

  • Identify the geometry and hybridization
  • Write the electronic configuration
  • Explain orbital overlap
  • Connect hybridization to geometry
  • Mention coordinate bond formation

Key Formulas

Square planar: dsp² hybridization; Coordinate bonds by orbital overlap
