Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.5

Original Question

Specify the oxidation numbers of the metals in the following coordination entities: (i) [Co(H₂O)(CN)(en)₂]²⁺ (ii) [CoBr₂(en)₂]⁺ (iii) [PtCl₄]²⁻ (iv) K₃[Fe(CN)₆] (v) [Cr(NH₃)₅Cl]²⁺

Background Concept — Counting Charges with Neutral/Anionic Ligands

Oxidation number is determined by balancing the overall charge with known ligand charges. Neutral ligands such as NH₃, H₂O, en, CO carry zero charge; anionic ligands such as Cl⁻, Br⁻, CN⁻, OH⁻ carry −1 (unless otherwise noted). For a complex cation/anions, sum of metal oxidation state and ligand charges equals the ion’s overall charge. For salts, include counter‑ions as needed to infer the complex ion’s charge before solving for the metal oxidation state.

Answer

(i) [Co(H₂O)(CN)(en)₂]²⁺: x + (0 − 1 + 0 + 0) = +2 → x = +3
(ii) [CoBr₂(en)₂]⁺: x + (−1 − 1 + 0 + 0) = +1 → x = +3
(iii) [PtCl₄]²⁻: x + 4(−1) = −2 → x = +2
(iv) K₃[Fe(CN)₆]: complex ion charge = −3, so x + 6(−1) = −3 → x = +3
(v) [Cr(NH₃)₅Cl]²⁺: x + (0×5) + (−1) = +2 → x = +3

Tips to Solve

  • Assign 0 to NH₃, H₂O, en; −1 to Cl⁻, Br⁻, CN⁻.
  • Sum ligand charges with metal oxidation to match overall ion charge.
  • For salts, deduce complex ion charge from counter‑ions first.
