Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.8

Original Question

List various types of isomerism possible for coordination compounds, giving an example of each.

Background Concept — Structural vs Stereoisomerism

Isomerism in coordination chemistry falls into structural isomerism (different connectivities) and stereoisomerism (same connectivity, different spatial arrangement). Structural types include ionization isomerism (exchange of inner/outer sphere ions), solvate/hydrate isomerism (exchange with solvent), coordination isomerism (exchange of ligands between cationic/anionic complexes), and linkage isomerism (ambidentate ligands bind through different donor atoms). Stereoisomerism includes geometrical isomerism (cis/trans, fac/mer) and optical isomerism (non‑superimposable mirror images, Δ/Λ for octahedral chelates). Recognizing these helps predict number of isomers and their properties (e.g., color, reactivity, optical activity).

Answer

  • Ionization: [Co(NH₃)₅SO₄]Br vs [Co(NH₃)₅Br]SO₄
  • Solvate/Hydrate: [Cr(H₂O)₆]Cl₃ vs [Cr(H₂O)₅Cl]Cl₂·H₂O
  • Coordination: [Co(NH₃)₆][Cr(CN)₆] vs [Cr(NH₃)₆][Co(CN)₆]
  • Linkage: [Co(NH₃)₅(NO₂)]²⁺ (nitro‑N) vs [Co(NH₃)₅(ONO)]²⁺ (nitrito‑O)
  • Geometrical: cis-/trans-[Pt(NH₃)₂Cl₂]; fac-/mer-[Co(NH₃)₃Cl₃]
  • Optical: Δ/Λ-[Co(en)₃]³⁺; [Cr(C₂O₄)₃]³⁻ (enantiomeric pair)

Tips to Solve

  • Group isomers into structural vs stereoisomerism.
  • Give one clean example per type.
