Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.7

Original Question

Using IUPAC norms write the formulas for the following: (i) Tetrahydroxidozincate(II) (ii) Potassium tetrachloridopalladate(II) (iii) Diamminedichloridoplatinum(II) (iv) Potassium tetracyanidonickelate(II) (v) Pentaamminenitrito-O-cobalt(III) (vi) Hexaamminecobalt(III) sulphate (vii) Potassium tri(oxalato)chromate(III) (viii) Hexaammineplatinum(IV) (ix) Tetrabromidocuprate(II) (x) Pentaamminenitrito-N-cobalt(III)

Background Concept — Parsing Names to Formulas

To convert an IUPAC name to a formula, identify the complex ion (cation or anion), list ligands with their counts and charges, determine the metal oxidation state from the name, then add counter‑ions to balance charge. For anionic complexes, metal names end in “‑ate,” often using Latin roots (e.g., nickelate, cuprate, ferrate). Ambidentate specification (nitrito‑O vs nitrito‑N) dictates the donor atom but not the overall formula, which remains NO₂ as the ligand unit. Multidentate ligands such as oxalate (oxalato, C₂O₄²⁻) use prefixes like di/tri inside parentheses when the ligand name already has prefixes. Finally, present salts with cation first (e.g., K⁺) followed by the complex anion in square brackets.

Answer

(i) [Zn(OH)₄]²⁻
(ii) K₂[PdCl₄]
(iii) [Pt(NH₃)₂Cl₂]
(iv) K₂[Ni(CN)₄]
(v) [Co(NH₃)₅(ONO)]³⁺
(vi) [Co(NH₃)₆]₂(SO₄)₃
(vii) K₃[Cr(C₂O₄)₃]
(viii) [Pt(NH₃)₆]⁴⁺
(ix) [CuBr₄]²⁻
(x) [Co(NH₃)₅(NO₂)]³⁺

Tips to Solve

  • For salts, place counter‑ions outside brackets to neutralize total charge.
  • Nitrito‑O is ONO; nitrito‑N is NO₂ as bound through N.
  • Check oxidation state consistency for each entry.
