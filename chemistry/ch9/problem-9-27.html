Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.27

Original Question

Briefly discuss, with an example for each, the role of coordination compounds in: (i) Biological systems (ii) Medicinal chemistry (iii) Analytical chemistry (iv) Extraction/metallurgy of metals.

Background Concept — Where Complexes Matter

Coordination compounds are pervasive across biology, medicine, analysis, and metallurgy. In biology, metal ions bound within specific ligand environments enable electron transfer, oxygen transport, and catalysis with exquisite selectivity—classic examples include hemoglobin/myoglobin (Fe–porphyrin) and vitamin B₁₂ (Co–corrin). In medicinal chemistry, metal complexes can target DNA or proteins: cisplatin and its analogues form covalent adducts with guanine bases, disrupting replication in rapidly dividing cells; other complexes serve as imaging agents (e.g., Gd³⁺ chelates for MRI) where chelation controls relaxivity and safety. In analytical chemistry, complexation underpins titrations (EDTA in complexometric assays for water hardness), masking agents in qualitative separations, and colorimetric/fluorimetric detection (e.g., Fe–phenanthroline). In extraction and metallurgy, selective complexation facilitates leaching, solvent extraction, and purification: cyanidation forms [Au(CN)₂]⁻ to dissolve gold; nickel is purified via volatile Ni(CO)₄ in the Mond process; extractants like oximes chelate Cu²⁺ for hydrometallurgical recovery. Complex stability, kinetics, and ligand design dictate selectivity and efficiency. Overall, coordination chemistry provides molecular handles to tune reactivity and transport of metal ions in diverse environments, making it central to technologies from catalysis to diagnostics.

Answer

  • Biological systems: O₂ transport by hemoglobin (Fe–porphyrin).
  • Medicinal chemistry: Cisplatin (Pt complex) as anticancer drug; Gd–DTPA in MRI.
  • Analytical chemistry: EDTA titration of Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ (water hardness).
  • Metallurgy: Cyanidation of Au via [Au(CN)₂]⁻; Mond process Ni(CO)₄.

Tips to Solve

  • Give one crisp example for each field; name the complex explicitly.
  • State the functional role (transport, therapy, assay, extraction).
