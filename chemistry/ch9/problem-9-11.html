Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.11

Original Question

Draw all the geometrical and optical isomers of: (i) [CoCl₂(en)₂]⁺ (ii) [Co(NH₃)Cl(en)₂]²⁺ (iii) [Co(NH₃)₂Cl₂(en)]⁺

Background Concept — Octahedral Isomerism with en

For octahedral complexes containing bidentate ligands like ethane‑1,2‑diamine (en), the presence and relative positions of identical ligands control geometrical (cis/trans) and optical (Δ/Λ) isomerism. Two en ligands lock chelate rings; when the remaining two sites are occupied by identical monodentates (e.g., 2Cl⁻), cis and trans arrangements are possible. cis forms lacking improper symmetry are typically chiral (Δ/Λ), while trans forms are usually achiral. For three chelate ligands (en) plus two different monodentates, multiple geometrical arrangements are reduced by chelation constraints; chirality depends on whether a mirror plane or inversion center persists.

Answer — Count and Types

  • (i) [CoCl₂(en)₂]⁺: cis (optically active; Δ and Λ) and trans (achiral). Total: 3 isomers (cis‑Δ, cis‑Λ, trans).
  • (ii) [Co(NH₃)Cl(en)₂]²⁺: only one geometrical arrangement around Co with two en and two different monodentates in cis positions; chiral (Δ/Λ). Total: 2 optical isomers.
  • (iii) [Co(NH₃)₂Cl₂(en)]⁺: cis and trans; cis is chiral (Δ/Λ), trans is achiral. Total: 3 isomers.
