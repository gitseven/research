Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.19

Original Question

Explain why [Cr(NH₃)₆]³⁺ is paramagnetic while [Ni(CN)₄]²⁻ is diamagnetic.

Background Concept — Geometry, Ligand Field, and Spin State

Magnetic behavior depends on the number of unpaired electrons, which is governed by metal d‑count, ligand field strength, and geometry. [Cr(NH₃)₆]³⁺ is octahedral with Cr³⁺ (d³); regardless of field strength, d³ occupies t₂g³ with three unpaired electrons → paramagnetic. [Ni(CN)₄]²⁻ is square‑planar with Ni²⁺ (d⁸); CN⁻ is a strong‑field, π‑acceptor ligand favoring large splitting. In square‑planar d⁸ complexes, electrons pair to fill the lower levels, leaving no unpaired electrons → diamagnetic. In contrast, tetrahedral [NiCl₄]²⁻ (weak field) is paramagnetic (two unpaired). Thus, both ligand identity and geometry dictate the spin state.

Answer

  • [Cr(NH₃)₆]³⁺: octahedral, Cr³⁺ (d³) → t₂g³ (3 unpaired) → paramagnetic.
  • [Ni(CN)₄]²⁻: square‑planar, Ni²⁺ (d⁸), CN⁻ strong field → fully paired → diamagnetic.
