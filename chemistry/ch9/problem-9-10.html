Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.10

Original Question

Draw the structures of optical isomers of: (i) [Cr(C₂O₄)₃]³⁻ (ii) [PtCl₂(en)₂]²⁺

Background Concept — Optical Activity in Octahedral Complexes

Optical isomerism arises when a complex lacks an internal plane of symmetry and exists as non‑superimposable mirror images (enantiomers). Octahedral complexes of the type [M(AA)₃] (AA = bidentate ligand such as oxalate or en) are classic: they form Δ and Λ configurations corresponding to right‑handed and left‑handed propeller twists of the chelate rings. Square planar complexes are generally achiral; however, octahedral [PtCl₂(en)₂]²⁺ can be chiral in its cis form (with two en ligands and two chlorido ligands) lacking improper symmetry, while the trans form is achiral.

Answer (schematic visuals)

(i) [Cr(C₂O₄)₃]³⁻: Δ and Λ enantiomers

(ii) [PtCl₂(en)₂]²⁺ (cis): Δ and Λ enantiomers

Tips to Solve

  • For [M(AA)₃], draw Δ/Λ propeller twists of chelate rings.
  • For [PtCl₂(en)₂]²⁺, only the cis isomer is chiral; trans is achiral.
