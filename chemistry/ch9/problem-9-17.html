Coordination Compounds · Problem 9.17

Original Question

What is spectrochemical series? Explain the difference between a weak field ligand and a strong field ligand.

Background Concept — Ligand Field Strength and Δ

The spectrochemical series orders ligands by the magnitude of crystal field splitting (Δ) they produce at a given metal/geometry. A typical octahedral series (weak → strong) is: I⁻ < Br⁻ < S²⁻ ≈ SCN⁻(S) < Cl⁻ < F⁻ < OH⁻ < H₂O < NH₃ < en < NO₂⁻(N) < CN⁻ < CO. Weak‑field ligands generate small Δ, favoring high‑spin configurations (electrons remain unpaired to minimize pairing energy). Strong‑field ligands produce large Δ, favoring low‑spin configurations (electrons pair in t₂g before occupying e_g). Field strength affects magnetic moments, colors (absorption energy), and thermodynamic/kinetic stability. π‑acceptor ligands (CO, CN⁻) enhance Δ via back‑bonding, while π‑donors (I⁻, Br⁻) reduce Δ.

Answer

  • Spectrochemical series: An empirical ordering of ligands by increasing Δ.
  • Weak field ligand: Small Δ → high‑spin, more unpaired electrons (e.g., I⁻, Br⁻, Cl⁻, F⁻, H₂O).
  • Strong field ligand: Large Δ → low‑spin, more pairing (e.g., NH₃, en, NO₂⁻(N), CN⁻, CO).

Tips to Solve

  • Quote a representative series fragment and define weak vs strong field via spin state.
  • Mention π‑acceptor/donor tendencies as a rationale.
