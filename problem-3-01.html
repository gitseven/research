Chapter 3 · Problem 3.1

Original Question

Arrange the following metals in the order in which they displace each other from the solution of their salts: Al, Cu, Fe, Mg and Zn.

Background Concept — Reactivity (Activity) Series

The ability of one metal to displace another from the solution of its salt is governed by the relative ease with which each metal is oxidised to its cation. This tendency is summarised in the activity (reactivity) series, which orders metals from the strongest reducing agents at the top to the weakest at the bottom. A metal that is higher in the series will spontaneously displace a metal that is lower from an aqueous solution of its ions. Thermodynamically, the driving force is captured by standard reduction potentials, E°(Mⁿ⁺/M). A more negative reduction potential means the reverse process (oxidation of M to Mⁿ⁺) is more favourable, and hence the metal acts as a stronger reducing agent. For displacement reactions of the type M₁ + M₂ⁿ⁺ → M₁ⁿ⁺ + M₂, the reaction will be spontaneous when E°(M₂ⁿ⁺/M₂) − E°(M₁ⁿ⁺/M₁) is positive. Kinetics (surface oxide layers, passivation, and complexation) can sometimes slow or inhibit observable displacement, but under typical aqueous conditions the E° ordering is a reliable predictor. In practical terms, highly electropositive metals such as alkali and alkaline earth metals readily reduce many cations, whereas coinage metals like copper and silver are reluctant to oxidise and therefore sit near the bottom of the activity series. For common first-row metals relevant here, standard potentials at 298 K indicate the following trend in reducing strength: Mg (very negative E°) > Al > Zn > Fe >> Cu (positive E°). Thus, magnesium will displace aluminium, zinc, iron, and copper; aluminium will displace zinc, iron, and copper; and so on. This conceptual framework directly translates into a simple rule-of-thumb for predicting single displacement reactions in aqueous solution and is foundational to corrosion science, galvanic series behaviour, and metal extraction processes.

Typical E° values (V): Mg −2.37, Al −1.66, Zn −0.76, Fe −0.44, Cu +0.34.
  • Rule of thumb: M₁ displaces M₂ from M₂-salt if M₁ is higher in the series (more reducing).

Answer

Order (most reactive → least): Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Cu.

Tips to Solve

  • Recall or quickly derive the activity series from standard reduction potentials E°(Mⁿ⁺/M).
  • More negative E° → stronger reducing agent → metal sits higher in displacement order.
  • When unsure, test a pairwise displacement: does M₁ + M₂ⁿ⁺ → M₁ⁿ⁺ + M₂ have E°cell > 0?
  • Watch for kinetic/passivation exceptions in real life; exam problems assume thermodynamic control.
