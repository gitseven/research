Q5. Standard Cell Potential Calculation

Given: E°(Zn²⁺/Zn) = -0.76 V, E°(Cu²⁺/Cu) = +0.34 V

E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode = E°(Cu²⁺/Cu) - E°(Zn²⁺/Zn) = 0.34 - (-0.76) = 1.10 V

Answer: 1.10 V

Q6. Variable Oxidation States in Transition Elements

Transition elements show variable oxidation states because:

  • They have incompletely filled d orbitals
  • The energy difference between (n-1)d and ns orbitals is small
  • Both s and d electrons can participate in bonding
  • Example: Mn shows +2, +3, +4, +6, +7 oxidation states
3

Q7. Integrated Rate Law for First-Order Reaction

Derivation: For first-order reaction A → B

Rate = -d[A]/dt = k[A]
-d[A]/[A] = k dt
∫[A]₀^[A] -d[A]/[A] = ∫₀^t k dt
ln([A]₀/[A]) = kt
[A] = [A]₀ e^(-kt)

Significance: The rate constant k has units s⁻¹ and represents the fraction of reactant that reacts per unit time.

3

Q8. Crystal Field Splitting in Octahedral Complexes

In octahedral complexes, d orbitals split into two sets:

  • t₂g orbitals: dxy, dxz, dyz (lower energy)
  • eg orbitals: dx²-y², dz² (higher energy)

The energy difference is called crystal field splitting energy (Δo). This splitting affects the color and magnetic properties of the complex.

5

Q9. Nernst Equation and Cell EMF

(a) Nernst Equation:

Ecell = E°cell - (RT/nF)lnQ
At 25°C: Ecell = E°cell - (0.0591/n)logQ

Significance: Relates cell potential to concentrations of reactants and products.

(b) Cell EMF Calculation:

E°cell = E°(Cu²⁺/Cu) - E°(Mg²⁺/Mg) = 0.34 - (-2.37) = 2.71 V
Q = [Mg²⁺]/[Cu²⁺] = 0.001/0.0001 = 10
Ecell = 2.71 - (0.0591/2)log(10) = 2.71 - 0.0296 = 2.68 V
5

Q10. Werner's Theory and Isomerism

(a) Werner's Theory:

  • Metal atoms have two types of valencies: primary (ionizable) and secondary (non-ionizable)
  • Primary valency is satisfied by anions
  • Secondary valency is satisfied by neutral molecules or negative ions
  • Secondary valency determines the geometry

(b) Cis and Trans Isomers:

Cis-[Pt(NH₃)₂Cl₂]: NH₃ and Cl are adjacent
Trans-[Pt(NH₃)₂Cl₂]: NH₃ and Cl are opposite

Q18. Compound Microscope

Q19. Lens Maker's Formula and Image Position

(a) Lens Maker's Formula:

1/f = (μ-1)(1/R₁ - 1/R₂)

Where μ is refractive index, R₁ and R₂ are radii of curvature.

(b) Image Position:

1/f₁ = 1/v₁ - 1/u₁
1/20 = 1/v₁ - 1/(-30) → v₁ = 60 cm
1/f₂ = 1/v₂ - 1/u₂
1/(-15) = 1/v₂ - 1/(60-30) → v₂ = -10 cm

Final image is 10 cm to the left of the concave lens.

5

(b) de Broglie Wavelength:

λ = h/p = h/(mv) = (6.63×10⁻³⁴)/(9.11×10⁻³¹ × 10⁶) = 7.27 × 10⁻¹⁰ m

Q5. Crystalline vs Amorphous Solids

Amorphous: Irregular arrangement, no sharp melting point, isotropic (e.g., glass, rubber)

Q6. Standard Electrode Potential

Standard electrode potential measures the tendency of an electrode to gain electrons. More positive values indicate greater tendency to be reduced (oxidizing agent).

Q7. Second Law of Thermodynamics

Statement: The entropy of the universe always increases in a spontaneous process.

Application: ΔG = ΔH - TΔS. For spontaneity, ΔG < 0. If ΔH < 0 and ΔS > 0, reaction is always spontaneous.
